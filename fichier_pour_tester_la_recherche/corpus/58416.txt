 Blue baby syndrome or blue baby is usually caused by a heart defect which laymen often call a hole in the heart Normally oxygen ated blood from the lungs is separated from deoxygenated blood from other tissues A defect in the heart ventricle walls can allow deoxygenated blood to mix with the blood from the lungs The resulting blood going through the aorta has less oxygen than usual and the baby looks blue An operation is usually done to fix this There are several heart defects which produce this syndrome There are also some causes which are not related to heart conditions On November the first successful operation for the heart condition was performed years ago at the Johns Hopkins Hospital
