 The Lynton Barnstaple Railway is a Heritage railway in North Devon England The original line opened in May but closed in It was a single track long running through rugged rural countryside in Exmoor National Park For a few years the line earned a small profit but for most of its life the L B lost money The L B was taken over by the Southern Railway Southern Railway in and finally closed in September The Lynton Barnstaple Railway Association was created in A short section of the line opened again in This was extended in and a year later plans were announced to open nine miles of track linking the station at Woody Bay to both Lynton and Blackmoor Gate and eventually to a new station at Wistlandpound Reservoir
