The Ubangi River also spelled Oubangui is a river of Central Africa It is the largest right bank tributary of the Congo River It begins where the Mbomou River Mbomou and Uele River s come together It flows west then bends to the southwest and passes through Bangui Then the river flows south to the Congo at Liranga The Ubangi s length is about The basin includes parts of the Democratic Republic of the Congo Central African Republic and Republic of Congo Together with the Congo River it important for transportation for river boats between Bangui and Brazzaville Some people want the water from the Ubangi to go to the Chari River The Chari River empties into Lake Chad They say this will help with fishing and make agriculture better
