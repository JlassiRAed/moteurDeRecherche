The Blue Plaque is a scheme for remembering the links between famous people and buildings by placing commemorative plaque s on the walls The scheme was started by the Royal Society of Arts in London and the very first plaques was in fact of red terracotta placed on the outside of a former home of Lord Byron The Society erected plaques between responsibility for them was transferred to the London County Council and most recently English Heritage Similar schemes are now operated in all the member countries of the United Kingdom
