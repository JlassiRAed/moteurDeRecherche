 Hyperland is a minute long documentary movie about hypertext and surrounding technologies It was written by Douglas Adams and produced and directed by Max Whitby It ran on BBC Two in It stars Douglas Adams as a computer user and Tom Baker as a personification of a software agent In the show Adams has a dream where he is browsing through various media While Adams is browsing many people and projects related to the general theme of hypertext and multimedia are presented The dream ends with a vision of how information might be accessed in Hyperland does describe a number of features of the modern web This is especially noteworthy because it predates the public release of the WorldWideWeb first Web browser by about a year
