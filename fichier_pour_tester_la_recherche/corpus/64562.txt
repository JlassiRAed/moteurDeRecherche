 Jaffa Gate is a stone arch way in the walls of the Old City of Jerusalem It is along one of the main roads that connects the western part of the city to the Western Wall and the Temple Mount above him The plaza of the Jaffa Gate replaced a tall Ancient Rome Roman pillar inside the Damascus Gate which was used as the Kilometer zero starting point for measurements to other cities in British Mandate of Palestine Palestine It later became the starting point used to measure the distances to other countries The Tower of David is a structure of the citadel and is used today as the History of Jerusalem Museum The road fro Jaffa Gate to the Western Wall called once the Capital street
