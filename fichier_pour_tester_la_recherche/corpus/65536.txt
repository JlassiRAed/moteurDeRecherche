The tooth fairy is a Mythology mythical Fictional character figure found in modern folklore Around the world families have many different traditions to celebrate the loss of a child s tooth especially a first tooth Many of the traditions involve throwing a tooth on a roof under a house burying it under a tree or leaving it for a rodent One of the most famous traditions is leaving a tooth under a pillow for the tooth fairy This tradition is very popular in the United States as well as other countries The tooth fairy is said to take the tooth away while the child is sleeping and leave some money in its place Sometimes the tooth fairy may also leave a note If the child does not go to sleep the tooth fairy will not come Some people tell the story that the tooth fairy is named Fatina and lives in Neverland The belief is that Fatina creates a magic powder from children s teeth that protects the fairies from hawk s who might otherwise eat them
