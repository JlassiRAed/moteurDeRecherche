A vacuum tube also called a valve in British English is an electronic device used in many older model radios television sets and amplifier s The cathode is heated as in a light bulb so it will emit electron s The anode is the part that accepts the emitted electrons The device may have other parts Vacuum tubes must be hot to work Most are made of glass thus are fragile and can break Vacuum tubes were used in the first computers like the ENIAC which were large and need much work to continue operating
