 For other uses see Wizard A wizard magician sorceror or sorceress is a fictional or mythical person who uses magic This ability to use magic may be due to innate ability training or both Wizards are often depicted as having a special connection to supernatural beings such as gods or Fairy fairies or as such supernatural beings themselves Merlin from the King Arthur Arthurian legends is one of the most well known wizard characters Wizards have been portrayed as both evil and Good are fictional examples of good wizards Voldemort and Saruman are corresponding examples of evil wizards Wizards are often depicted as old men with long flowing beards and wearing a large brimmed hat a thick cloak and holding a staff This may be due to descriptions of Odin the Wanderer a god from Norse mythology who often disguised himself thus in order to pass through the lands of men unnoticed Wizards when they are named thus are almost always portrayed as male but some fictional or mythical female characters seem to play the same role sometimes as a Foil foil character to a male wizard or other character Notable examples include Morgan le Fay from Arthurian legends and Circe from Greek mythology Nowadays the term wizard is also used for someone whose accomplishments are due to unusual skill or creativity Thomas Edison the noted United States American inventor was nicknamed the Wizard of Menlo Park
