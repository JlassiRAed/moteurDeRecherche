 Theodore Roethke was a prominent American poet who wrote intensely personal sensitive and spiritualistic poetry Roethke s parents were German people German immigrants who settled in Michigan and started a highly prosperous floral business He was born in Michigan He grew up in an atmosphere of greenhouses and plants These images stayed with him and he often alluded to it in his work He was educated at the Michigan State University and then Harvard He became a teacher and sports coach He began to publish his poetry later around with the publication of Open House However according to critics and scholars he really began to mature with his second book The Lost Son and Other Poems which was followed by other critically acclaimed volumes Roethke joined the University of Washington as a lecturer in He stayed there until his sudden death in Among other prizes and awards he won the Pulitzer Prize
