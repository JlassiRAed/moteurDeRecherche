In academic administration a dean is a person with important authority over a specific Academia academic unit or over a specific area of concern or both The term comes from the Latin decanus a leader of ten taken from the Middle Ages medieval Monastery monasteries The monks were organized into groups of ten for administrative purposes along the lines of military platoon s headed by a senior monk the decanus The term was later used to describe the head of a community of priests as the chapter of a cathedral or a section of a diocese When the University universities grew out of the cathedral and monastery schools the title of dean was used for officials with various administrative duties
