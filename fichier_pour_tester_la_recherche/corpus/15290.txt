 Symphony No in F major Op and excerpts from the Mass in C major of There are five movements in the symphony The movements in order are allegro ma non troppo The third movement is the scherzo movement of the symphony It depicts the country folk dancing and it ends unexpectedly when the country folk notice that raindrops are starting to fall The fourth movement depicts a violent thunderstorm starting with just a few drops of rain and building to a climax There is of course thunder as well as lightning high winds and sheets of rain The finale is in sonata rondo form The first eight bars form a continuation of the introduction of which the storm was the main part the finale proper begins in the ninth bar
