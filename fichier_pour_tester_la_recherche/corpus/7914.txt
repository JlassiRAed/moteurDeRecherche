In Anglican church music a Service is a piece of music for the choir to sing during a service of worship It uses the words which are a traditional part of the service The choir is usually accompaniment accompanied by the pipe organ organ but sometimes it can be just for choir without organ In Morning Prayer or Benedictus For a Eucharist Holy Communion the set words will usually be the Gloria sometimes the Creed Sanctus Agnus Dei as well as responses For Evening Prayer Evening Prayer the words will be the Magnificat and the Nunc Dimittis Singers in the choir often call these Mag and Nunc for short Many composer s have set these words They make them into a piece for choir which is like an anthem In the Tudor period Tudor and early Stuart periods services were described as Short Great or Verse services Short services were short the words were only sung once and it was often unaccompanied solo choir member In between each verse there is some music for the whole choir to sing
