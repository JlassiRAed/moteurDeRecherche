 Ferdinand Julius Cohn was a Germans German biologist He is a founder of modern bacteriology and microbiology Cohn was born in the Jewish quarter of Breslau in the Kingdom of Prussia Prussian Province of Silesia This is now Wroclaw Poland Starting at age he studied botany under Heinrich Goppert at the University of Breslau He then moved to the University of Berlin At age in he received a degree in botany at Berlin In he returned to the University of Breslau and he remained at that university for the rest of his career as a teacher and researcher His father had bought for him a large and expensive microscope which the University of Breslau and most universities did not have at that time They had microscopes but not so good as his It was Ferdinand Cohn s main research tool in the s In the s he studied the growth and division of plant cells In he produced papers on the sexuality of Sphaeroplea and later Volvox In the s he studied plant physiology Cohn was the first to classify algae as plant s and to define what distinguishes them from green plants From onward he mostly studied bacteria He published over research reports during his lifetime The University of Breslau became an innovative center for plant physiology and microbiology while he was there His classification of bacteria into four groups based on shape is still in use today Among other things Cohn showed that Bacillus can change from a vegetative state to an endospore when conditions are hard Heat resistant spores of B subtilis caused a controversy over spontaneous generation Cohn explained why boiled infusions of hay and cheese could still get microbial growth It was because they contained heat resistant spores
