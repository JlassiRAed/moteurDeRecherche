 The Defense Intelligence Agency or DIA is the central producer and manager of foreign military intelligence for the United States As one of the principal members of the U S Intelligence Community the agency works to answer national level defense objectives for the President of the United States President the United States Secretary of Defense Secretary of Defense and senior U S policymakers Although the DIA is designated a United States Department of Defense Department of Defense combat support agency the majority of its employees espionage activities around the world particularly in countries where the military has better access or more specialized military experts than the Central Intelligence Agency Further DIA leads the Intelligence Community in collection and production of measurement and signature intelligence The agency was established in under President John F Kennedy by U S Secretary of Defense Defense Secretary Robert McNamara The Director of the Defense Intelligence Agency who is nominated by the President of the United States President and confirmed by the Senate of the United States Senate chairs the Military Intelligence Board which coordinates activities of the entire United States Intelligence Community Organization defense intelligence community
