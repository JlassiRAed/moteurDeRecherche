In geometry perimeter is the distance around a flat object For example all four sides of a square rhombus have the same length so a rhombus with side length inches would have a perimeter of inches Real life objects have perimeters as well A football field including the end zones is feet long and feet wide So the perimeter of the field is feet The perimeter of a circle is usually called the circumference It may be calculated by multiplying the diameter times Pi Pi is a constant which is equal to however the places to the right of the decimal are endless The number of places used depend on the accuracy required for the result
