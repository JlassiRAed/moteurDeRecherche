The little owl and North Africa It was introduced into Britain at the end of the nineteenth century and into the South Island of New Zealand in the early twentieth century This owl is a member of the Strigidae which contains most species of owl The other group is the barn owls Tytonidae The little owl is a small camouflage cryptically coloured mainly nocturnal species It is found in a range of habitats including farmland woodland fringes steppe s and semi deserts It feeds on insects earthworms other invertebrates and small vertebrates Males have territories which they defend against intruders This owl is a cavity nester and a clutch of about four eggs is laid in spring The female does the incubation and the male brings food to the nest first for the female and later for the newly hatched young As the chicks grow both parents hunt and bring them food and the chicks leave the nest at about seven weeks of age There are thirteen recognized subspecies of little owl spread across Europe and Asia
