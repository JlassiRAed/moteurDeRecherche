 Quetzals are brightly colored birds in the trogon family They are found in forests and woodlands especially in humid highlands These birds live in the Americas The word quetzal was first used for the resplendent quetzal a famous long tailed quetzal that lives in Central America The resplendent quetzal is the national symbol of Guatemala They are strongly sexual dimorphism sexually dimorphic as usual with the males being brighter Parts of the female plumageis brown or grey These largely solitary birds feed on fruits berries insects and small vertebrates Even with their famous bright plumage they can be hard to see in their natural wooded habitats In Aztec times the quetzal was worshiped as the god of the air Its long tail feathers which can grow up to ft long were highly prized for making into costume and head dresses
