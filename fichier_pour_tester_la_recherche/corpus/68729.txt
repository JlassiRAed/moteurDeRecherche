 The Hall of Mirrors in the palace of Versailles France was constructed on what was once a terrace designed by Le Vau Mansart and Le Brun designed and built the Hall between and The Hall is decorated with marble The French order wikt capital Noun capitals depict a cock Apollo and fleur de lys The ceiling depicts the War with Holland They also depict the administrative reforms in the early years of Louis XIV s reign Two of the four alabaster tables are from the collections of the duc d Antin The vases and busts are from the Royal collections The chandeliers and candelabra were reconstructed in after the originals and documents in the archives During the th century the Hall of Mirrors was used by Louis XIV when he walked from his private apartment to the chapel At this time courtiers assembled to watch the king and members of the royal family pass In the Hall was the scene of The Yew Tree Ball It was at this event that Louis XV met Madame de Pompadour In the th century the Prussian king William I was declared German emperor on January in the Hall of Mirrors by Bismarck and the victorious German princes and lords French Prime Minister Clemenceau chose the Hall of Mirrors to sign the Treaty of Versailles that ended World War I on June The Hall of Mirrors still serves for state occasions such as receptions for visiting heads of state
