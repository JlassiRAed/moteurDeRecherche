The loudness war is a negative term that describes the apparent competition to release Album albums that are increasingly loud Albums that are victims of the loudness war have less good sound quality because the dynamic range has been compressed to make the music louder Examples of albums that are victims of the loudness war include Death Magnetic by Metallica Record producer Charles Dye started an organization called Turn Me Up that campaigns to give musicians the choice to make albums with bigger dynamic range dynamic ranges In March mastering engineer Ian Shepherd organised the first Dynamic Range Day
