 Gastroliths or gizzard stones are small Rock rocks kept by an animal inside its stomach or at an early part of the gastrointestinal tract alimentary canal The function of these is to mash up the food so digestion takes place faster Typically animals which do this do not have suitable teeth for wikt grinding grinding and the gastroliths are a good substitute for this A second function in aquatic animals is that gastroliths can be used for ballast to make diving easier
