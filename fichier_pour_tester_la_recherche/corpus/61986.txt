An ovipositor is the long hollow tube on the rear of female insects Through this tube they lay eggs The ovipositor is formed by the union of three pairs of rear appendage s In many species it is a piercing organ which allows the insect to put its eggs into a specific place In some species of wasp the female drill s through tree bark with her ovipositor and lays eggs in the bodies of Larva grubs eating under the bark However in many hymenoptera the ovipositor is used as a Sting sting and the female does not use it for egg laying
