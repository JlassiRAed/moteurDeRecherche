The Grampian Mountains or Grampians are one of the three major mountain ranges in Scotland occupying a considerable portion of the Scottish Highlands in northeast Scotland They occupy almost half the land area of Scotland The Grampians extend southwest to northeast between the Highland Boundary Fault The mountains are composed of granite and metamorphic rocks such as gneiss marble schist s and quartz ite The other major mountain ranges in Scotland are the Northwest Highlands and the Southern Uplands
