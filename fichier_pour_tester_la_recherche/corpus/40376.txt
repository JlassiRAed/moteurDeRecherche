 The Jew of Malta is a play The works of Christopher Marlowe Volume pp xxxix xliii The play is unbalanced the first two acts show a masterful hand while the last three do not The character of Barabas the Jew is a portrait of depth in the first two acts but he descends to nothing more than a typical stage villain in the last three One critic believes the play was ordered on short notice by the players and Marlowe had only enough time to sketch the character of Barrabas
