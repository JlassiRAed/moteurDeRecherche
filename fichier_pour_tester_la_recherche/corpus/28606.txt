An ecoregion the boundaries of an ecoregion approximate the original extent of the natural communities prior to any major recent disruptions or changes The World Wide Fund for Nature WWF has identified terrestrial ecoregions and approximately freshwater ecoregions across the Earth World Wide Fund for Nature s full definition of an ecoregion which is widely accepted and used is the following A large area of land or water that contains a geographically distinct assemblage of natural communities that share a large majority of their species and ecological dynamics share similar environmental conditions and interact ecologically in ways that are critical for their long term persistence World Wide Fund for Nature Ecoregions The Global is the list of ecoregions identified by the World Wide Fund for Nature as priorities for conservation
