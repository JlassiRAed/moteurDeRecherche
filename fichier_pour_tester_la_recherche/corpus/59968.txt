 Tjunkaya Tapaya is an Indigenous Australians Aboriginal Australia n Aboriginal art artist She is most recognised for her batik work and is one of the most well known batik artists in Australia Most of Tapaya s paintings depict places and events from her family s Dreamtime dreaming stories Her batik work is of the classic Ernabella style which eschews the Indonesian use of repeated block printed designs in favour of hand drawn freehand designs or walka These walka are pure design and do not refer to or contain reference to dreamings or tjukurpa Tjunkaya is a member of the Pitjantjatjara people She was born in the desert in the far northwest of South Australia Her mother had walked from Walytjitjata in the Northern Territory and arrived at Ernabella Mission at Pukatja or Pukatja South Australia Ernabella shortly after Tjunkaya s birth Ernabella was run by Mission missionaries at the time and Tjunkaya grew up there The craft room at Ernabella Mission was established in and nurtured the artistic talents of Tjunkaya and other children and adults living on the Mission The craft room taught spinning and weaving originally but later added batik and painting to its activities and later still became a professionally run Aboriginal owned incorporated arts enterprise known as Ernabella Arts after the Mission was closed and the land returned to its traditional Pitjantjatjara owners Like most early artists in her community Tjunkaya s original style came from milpatjunanyi ndash an ancient Western Desert cultural bloc Western Desert practice of drawing in the sand to tell stories to children This is the basis of the classic Ernabella walka Ernabella women began to be taught batik making in In Ernabella Arts sent Tjunkaya to Yogyakarta with several other Pitjantjatjara women to learn more about making batik from Indonesian artists Tjunkaya s work has been shown in several Art exhibition exhibitions in Australia Europe North America and Southeast Asia
