An ethnic conflict is a conflict where different ethnic group s fight each other It is different from a civil war where a single nation or ethnic group is fighting among itself Examples of ethnic wars since the s were typically caused by secession ist movements leading to the breakup of multi ethnic states along ethnic lines the Yugoslav Wars the First Chechen War the Nagorno Karabakh War the Rwandan Civil War and the War in Darfur among othersAcademic explanations of ethnic conflict generally fall into one of three schools of thought primordialist instrumentalist or constructivist Intellectual debate has also focused around the issue of whether ethnic conflict has become more common since the end of the Cold War and on finding ways of managing conflicts through instruments such as consociationalism and federalisation
