 Charles Wood was an Ireland Irish composer and teacher Charles Wood was born in Armagh Ireland His father sang in the Cathedral choir Charles had music lessons from the cathedral Organ horn and piano After four years he went to Selwyn College Cambridge at first to study then to teach harmony and counterpoint In he got a teaching job at Gonville and Caius College Cambridge Soon he became their first Director of Music and Organist When Stanford died Wood became Professor of Music at University of Cambridge Cambridge Wood is mainly remembered today for his Church of England Anglican church music He wrote music for the Mass service setting s of the Magnificat and Nunc dimittis These are often sung today in cathedrals and churches He wrote many fine anthem s including O thou the central orb In Cambridge he is remembered for the chimes he wrote for the clocks of Gonville and Caius College His pupils included Ralph Vaughan Williams at Cambridge and Herbert Howells at the Royal College of Music
