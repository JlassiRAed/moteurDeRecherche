 The Mackenzie River is a major river in northern Canada It is named after famous explorer Alexander Mackenzie The river s watershed drains parts of Alberta British Columbia the Northwest Territories Saskatchewan and the Yukon It flows north for from Great Slave Lake to the Arctic Ocean There are only a few small towns on the river including Fort Providence and Norman Wells The river is used as a boat transport route during the ice free summer Tributaries of the river include the Liard Keele Great Bear Arctic Red and Peel Rivers The Slave River Peace River Peace River and Athabasca River provide most of the water in Great Slave Lake
