The peso is the monetary unit of some Spanish speaking countries They were once Spanish colonies They include Argentina Chile Colombia Cuba Dominican Republic Mexico Philippines and Uruguay The word means weight in Spanish or more loosely pound It was a coin that originated in Spain and became of immense importance internationally Spain brought it to its colony colonies and mint ed many pesos in Mexico and Peru Later Spain stopped using this name but some of the former colonies still use it Historically the peso was eight reales the real de a ocho This translates to the well known English term pieces of eight It is known in many countries as the thaler or Spanish dollar It was used as the model for the original United States dollar and many other coins which were also minted in silver
