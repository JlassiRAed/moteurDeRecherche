 Wild type is a term in genetics for the most common type in a natural population It is applied to allele s of a gene or the genotype s or phenotype s of a species The term is used in genetics to contrast normal alleles with Mutation mutant alleles With Drosophila melanogaster wild type usually means the standard version of the famous laboratory population used in the T H Morgan laboratory and since used around the world This was of course only a Sample sample of the natural populations
