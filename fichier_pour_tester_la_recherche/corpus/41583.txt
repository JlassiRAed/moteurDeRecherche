A nostril is one of the two channels of the nose from the point where they to the external opening In bird s and mammal s they contain branched bones or cartilages called turbinate s whose function is to warm air on inhalation and remove moisture on exhalation Fish do not breathe through their noses but they do have two small holes used for olfaction smelling which may be called nostrils In human s the nasal cycle means that during the course of a day the nostrils will switch over approximately every four hours or so meaning that only one nostril is used at any one time
