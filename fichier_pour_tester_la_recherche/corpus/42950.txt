 Channel drift also called network decay is when a television channel moves away from its original shows to get a new audience or to get higher ratings It often involves a move from shows aimed at viewers that know a lot about the channel s subject to shows aimed at a wider audience Most examples of channel drift have a channel add infotainment or reality television reality shows to their schedule A well known example of channel drift is MTV When it started in MTV was a channel that showed music video s In the s it started adding entertainment and reality shows aimed at a young adult audience This was the start of a move towards the channel s current focus on shows aimed at adolescence adolescent s and young adults
