 Zhuyin Fuhao often abbreviated zhuyin and colloquially called bopomofo In Chinese language Chinese bo po mo and fo are the first four of the conventional ordering of available syllable s As a result the four syllables together have been used to indicate various phonetics phonetic systems For Chinese speakers who were first introduced to the Zhuyin system bopomofo means zhuyin fuhao The zhuyin characters are represented in typographic typeface fonts as if drawn with an ink brush They are encoded in Unicode in the bopomofo block in the range U U D
