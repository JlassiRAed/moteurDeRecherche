 Acherontia atropos A atropos Acherontia styx A styx Acherontia lachesis A lachesis The name Death s head hawkmoth is used for three species types of moth The and are in the genus Acherontia The most common usage of the name is for the Europe n species A atropos is usually found in Europe but A styx and A lachesis are found in Asia You can spot them by the skull shaped pattern of markings on the thorax All three species are about the same in size coloration and life cycle These moths have many unusual features All three species can make a loud squeak if irritated The sound is made by moving air from the pharynx often along with the flashing of the brightly colored abdomen in a further attempt to deter predators All three species are commonly seen raiding beehives of different species of honey bee for honey but A atropos only attacks colonies of the well known western honey bee Apis mellifera They are able to move about in hives unhurt because they mimic the scent of the bees
