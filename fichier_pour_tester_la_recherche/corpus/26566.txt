The Steppe Geoglyphs are a number of Neolithic earth constructions in the Turgay Trough area of Turgai in northern Kazakhstan There are at least of these earthworks Many or all of them are smaller earthworks These shapes are squares rings and three others Besides being made of earth dug out and piled up some of the geoglyphs are made by placing stones next to each other Some of the large shapes have been given names including Bestamskoe Ring Ushtogaysky Square Turgay triradial swastika Large cross Ashtasti Ekedyn cross Ashutasti ring Kyzyloba line Koga cross and Shili square Optical dating was used to get a date for these structures The results indicate that they date between eight and one thousand years ago The earthworks were discovered in by Dimitriy Dey
