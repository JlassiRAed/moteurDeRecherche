A Bicameral system of government is one where there are two legislative or parliament ary chambers In most cases they have different numbers of members The smaller legislature is often called the upper house or senate and the larger is called the lower house and sometimes also called by another name such as assembly or commons For legislation to be passed bicameral legislatures normally need a majority of members of both chambers to vote for the legislation Experts sometimes point out that often deadlock s occur in this kind of system
