 Statistics uses variable s to describe a measurement Such a variable is called significant if the probability that its outcome was obtained by chance is less than a given value Statistical hypothesis test s are used to check significance The concept of statistical significance was originated by Ronald Fisher when he developed statistical hypothesis testing which he described as tests of significance in his publication Statistical Methods for Research Workers Despite his initial suggestion of as a significance level Fisher did not intend this cutoff value to be fixed and in his publication Statistical methods and scientific inference he recommended that significant levels be set according to specific circumstances
