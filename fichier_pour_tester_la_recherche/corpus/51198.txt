A fen is one of the four main types of wetland and is usually fed by mineral rich surface water or groundwater Fens have a characteristic set of plant species which sometimes provide the best indicators of environmental conditions They are called fen indicator species Fens are distinguished from bog s which are acid ic low in minerals and usually dominated by sedges and shrubs with abundant Sphagnum mosses Fens have been damaged in the past by land drainage and also by peat cutting The principal challenges are to restore natural water flow regimes to maintain the quality of water and to prevent invasion by woody plants
