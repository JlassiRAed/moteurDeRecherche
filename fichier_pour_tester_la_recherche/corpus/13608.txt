 Flying fish are Marine biology marine oceanic fishes of the family Exocoetidae They are about species and they live worldwide in warm water s They are noted for their ability to glide They are all small with a maximum length of about cm and have wing like rigid fins and an unevenly forked tail They do not fly actively their fins do not flap What they do is speed towards the surface and keep on going When they leave the water they glide sometimes for a surprising distance It is generally thought to be an adaptation which helps them escape predators The earliest known flying fish were in the Middle Triassic New flying fish discovered in China BBC Nature News Xu et al A new stem neopterygian fish from the Middle Triassic of China shows the earliest over water gliding strategy of the vertebrates Proc R Soc B
