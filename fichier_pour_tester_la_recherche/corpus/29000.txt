In geology a bed is the smallest division of Rock separating it from layers above and below A bed is the smallest stratigraphy lithostratigraphic unit It ranges in thickness from a centimetre to several metres It can be distinguished from beds above and below it by rock or mineral type and particle size The term is usually applied to sedimentary Stratum strata but may also be used for volcanic flows or Volcanic ash ash layers In a quarry bedding is a term used for a structure occurring in granite and similar rocks which allows them to split in well defined planes horizontally or parallel to the land surface
