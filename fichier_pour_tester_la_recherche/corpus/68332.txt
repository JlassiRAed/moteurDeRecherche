The Eureka Stockade is the name given to a rebellion by gold miner s at Ballarat Victoria Australia It took place at sunrise on December The miners refused to pay a licence fee to look for gold The miners said that the money for the licence was a tax and that if they had to pay tax then they should be represented in the parliament Irish people Irish miner Peter Lalor became the leader of the miners They built a fort or stockade on Bakery Hill above the gold diggings on the Eureka gold lead They were attacked by the British Army More than people were killed The battle went for minutes
