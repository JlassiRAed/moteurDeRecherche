The volume of an object is a measure of the amount of Space space occupied by that object not to be confused with mass The volume of a mountain is much larger than the volume of a rock for instance The word volume implies a D three dimensional context where by convention For objects at or near the Earth s surface height or depth often refers to the dimension of the object along the local vertical All physical objects occupy a volume even if some are so thin that they appear to be D two dimensional like a sheet of paper
