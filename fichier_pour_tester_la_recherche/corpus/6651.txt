 William Lawson Blue Mountains from Sydney Lawson was born in Finchley Middlesex England He is the son of Scottish parents Lawson came to Sydney as an ensign with the New South Wales Corps in November He was soon posted to Norfolk Island After Lawson was appointed aide de camp to George Johnston at Prospect New South Wales Prospect He built a room mansion there In Lawson with Gregory Blaxland and William Wentworth led a trip west from Sydney to cross the Blue Mountains They left on May with four servants five dogs and four horses The route they travelled is still the one used by travellers today On May they reached Mount Blaxland and could see the plains to the west Lawson was rewarded with a grant of acres In Governor Lachlan Macquarie made a new settlement Bathurst New South Wales Bathurst He made Lawson commander of the Bathurst settlement Lawson had this job until From Bathurst Lawson explored as far north as Mudgee After leaving the army Lawson was a member of the New South Wales Legislative Council from to He died at Veteran Hall on June The town of Lawson New South Wales Lawson in the Blue Mountains is named for him In Lawson was honoured together with Blaxland and Wentworth on a postage stamp issued by Australia Post showing the Blue Mountains crossing
