In theatre blocking is the exact positioning of actor s on a Stage stage during a performance The word comes from practices used by theatre director s of the th century who worked out the staging of a scene on a Scale model model stage using wooden blocks to represent each of the actors Today the director usually determines blocking during rehearsal They tell the actors where they should move for the proper dramatic and light ing effect and to ensure that the audience can see everything The stage itself has been given named areas to help blocking The back of the stage is considered up stage The front of the stage is down stage Stage left and right refer to the actor s left and right facing the audience Because this is sometimes misunderstood the terms prompt are also used House left and house right refer to how the audience sees the stage The audience s left is called house left and the audience s right is called house right
