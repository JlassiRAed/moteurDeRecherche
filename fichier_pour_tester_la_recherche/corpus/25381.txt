 African Sanctus is a musical composition for choir soloists orchestra and prerecorded Compact Cassette tape by David Fanshawe It was composed in African Sanctus is the best known of Fanshawe s works It was composed after Fanshawe had made journeys to Africa up the river Nile between and The shape of his journey forms the shape of a cross This had a religious meaning for Fanshawe He made recordings on tape of the people he met singing their traditional music A lot of this music had never been recorded before When he returned home to Great Britain Britain he composed the African Sanctus Some of the recordings he had made in Africa are played as part of the performance The choir who sing live at the concert are singing some of the words of the traditional Christian Latin language Latin Mass Mass The recordings are from Egypt the Sudan Uganda and Kenya At first it was called African Revelations but then the title was changed to African Sanctus It was first performed in London by the Saltarello Choir in July and was later played on BBC Radio on United Nations Day A documentary film was made three years later showing how the work had been composed
