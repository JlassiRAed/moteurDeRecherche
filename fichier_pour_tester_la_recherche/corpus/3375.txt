 Geobox River National River Flow Archive CEH Wallingford The River Trent is one of the major rivers of England It is the third longest river in the United Kingdom It starts in Staffordshire It flows through the centre of England until it joins the River Ouse to form the River Humber which empties into the North Sea The name Trent comes from a Celtic languages Celtic word possibly meaning strongly flooding More specifically the name may be a contraction of two Celtic words tros It is unusual amongst English rivers in that it flows north and is also unusual in exhibiting a tidal bore the Aegir The area drained by the river includes most of the northern Midlands
