 Brave and Bold is a boys book written by Horatio Alger Jr It was serialized beginning on August in the New York Weekly Also published in the same issue was Alger s poem Friar Anselmo Alger had lost some of his steam as the s approached He had made his literary name in exposing the horrors of life for street children in books such as Ragged Dick As the years passed the government had taken on these horrors studies were being conducted and the plight of street children was being relieved As a result Alger was left without a literary subject Boys tastes were changing They knew everything they needed to know about street boys from Alger s many novels about the poor They wanted cowboys and Indians Alger s publisher and his editor urged him ramp up the excitement in his books Alger agreed but let it be known that his moralizing would remain at its usual high level Brave and Bold was the first in a new series of Alger novels published by Loring and the first in which sex rears its head Before Brave and Bold the girls of the hero s age were sisters or simply prop figures In this new book Hester Paine the lovely daughter of Millville s most prominent citizen and the reigning village belle becomes a source of fascination and contention for factory boy hero Robert Rushton and his nemesis the rich snobbish kid glove wearing youth Halbert Davis Brave and Bold hit a new high in Alger s work according Edwin Hoyt but Hoyt describes the story as a fiasco
