 Host defence peptides are part of the Innate immune system innate immune response They are found among all classes of life They act against invading microorganisms The peptides kill Gram negative and Gram positive bacteria This includes strains which are antibiotic resistance resistant to conventional antibiotics The peptides also work against Mycobacterium mycobacteria enveloped viruses fungi and even transformed or cancer ous cells These peptides are excellent candidates for medical use They complement conventional antibiotic therapy They have a broad range of activity and are bactericidal as opposed to bacteriostatic They need only a short contact time to kill bacteria There are big differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells as targets for antimicrobial peptides These peptides are potent broad spectrum antibiotic s Unlike most antibiotics it seems that antimicrobial peptides may also improve immunity by working as immunomodulator s
