The Euston Arch was an arch way built at the front of Euston railway station in London It was built in by the London and Birmingham Railway The people in charge of the new and modern railway wanted passenger s to have a good feeling about the station They built the large arch as a gate in front of the station to attract attention Four column s supported the arch The outside two columns were plain and square The inside two were circular and decoration decorated in the Doric order Doric style This Doric style gave the arch its common name of The Doric Arch Originally the words London and Birmingham Railway marked the top of the gate In the letters EUSTON in gold were carved into the beam across the tops of the four columns This railway company had joined other companies to form the London and North Western Railway This new company did not want passengers to use the old name It changed the name of the arch from the company name to the station name
