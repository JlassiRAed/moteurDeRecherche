 Epicrionops Rhinatrema Rhinatrematidae is the family of neotropical tailed caecilian s or beaked caecilians They are found in the equatorial countries of South America They are usually said to be the most primitive of the caecilian families They have many features other groups do not For example they still have a tail and the mouth is not to the back on the underside of the head They lay their eggs in small holes in the soil The larvae have external gills They live in seepages until they change into adults The adults live in moist soil and leaf litter
