A strait is a narrow channel of water that connects two land massesIt usually means a channel of water that is between two land masses It may also mean a narrow channel of water which is navigable in a body of water which is otherwise unpassable The terms strait channel passage sound and firth can be synonym ous but each is sometimes used with a slight difference of meaning Many straits are economically important Straits may lie on important shipping route s and war s have been fought for control of these straits Many artificial channels called canal s have been constructed to connect two bodies of water over land Although rivers and canals often form a bridge between two large lakes or a lake and a sea they are not usually referred to as straits Straits are typically much larger wider structures that do not have water running in a single direction and normally connect two seas
