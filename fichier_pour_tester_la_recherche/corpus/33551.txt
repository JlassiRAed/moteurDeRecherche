A Roman consul was the highest elected political office of the Roman Republic Each year two consuls were elected together to serve for a one year term Each consul was given veto power over his colleague and the officials would alternate each month The consuls were usually patrician s though after BC pleb s could stand for election as consul Consuls had extensive power in peacetime and in wartime often held the highest military command They conducted some religious rites which could only be done by the highest state officials Consuls also read Augury auguries an essential step before leading armies into the field
