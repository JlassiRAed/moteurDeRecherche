 Caracara are birds of prey in the family Falconidae They live in South America South and Central America and the southern USA Mostly they are not fast fliers and are scavenger s One species the red throated caracara eats insects and the larvae of bees and wasps Their classification is somewhat unclear but some sources say there are five living genera and species They were all originally put within the genus Polyborus Extinct species have been found in the La Brea tar pits
