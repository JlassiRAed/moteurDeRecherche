 Keble College is one of the colleges of the University of Oxford in England Its main buildings are on Parks Road opposite the University Museum and the University Parks Keble is one of the larger colleges with undergraduates and graduate students The head of the college is the Warden and senior academic members are Fellows Keble was founded in having been built as a monument to John Keble a leading member of the Oxford Movement As originally constituted it was for men only and the fellows were mostly bachelors resident in the college In the period after the second World War the trends were towards scientific courses and eventually co education for men and women from onwards It remains distinctive for its once controversial neo gothic red brick buildings designed by William Butterfield During the th century the college has been extended several times by new buildings
