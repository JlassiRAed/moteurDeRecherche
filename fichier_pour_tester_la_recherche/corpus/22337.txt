The United Fruit Company was an United States American corporation It dealt with tropical fruits especially bananas These fruits were grown on Central America Central and South America South American plantations They were sold in the United States and Europe The company was created in from Minor C Keith s banana trading concerns with Andrew W Preston s Boston Fruit Company It grew in the early and mid th century It controlled vast territories and transportation networks in Central America the Caribbean Sea Caribbean coast of Colombia Ecuador and the Caribbean West Indies It competed with the Standard Fruit Company in the international banana trade It maintained a monopoly in certain regions that came to be called banana republic s It had a deep and long lasting impact on the economy and politics of many Latin American countries Critics often accused it of neocolonialism They described it as the example of the influence of a multinational corporation on the internal politics of countries After a period of financial decline United Fruit merged with Eli M Black s AMK in to become the United Brands Company In Carl Lindner Jr transformed United Brands into the present day Chiquita Brands International
