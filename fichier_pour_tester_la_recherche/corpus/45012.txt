 Peter Ware Higgs CH Fellow of the Royal Society FRS Royal Society of Edinburgh FRSE is an English people English Theoretical physics theoretical physicist and emeritus professor at the University of Edinburgh He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for predicting the existence of the Higgs boson the most sought after particle in modern physics In the s he proposed broken symmetry in electroweak theory This explains the origin of mass of elementary particle s in general It also shows the origin of the W and Z bosons The theory known as Higgs mechanism was proposed by several physicists at the same time It proposes the existence of a new particle the Higgs boson On July CERN announced they had found a new particle much like the Higgs boson in experiment s The Higgs mechanism is generally accepted as an important part of the Standard Model of particle physics without which particles would have no mass Higgs has received a number of awards These include the Dirac Medal and Prize for outstanding contributions to theoretical physics the Wolf Prize in Physics and the Sakurai Prize
