 Bodmin is a town on the A road in Cornwall United Kingdom It is situated in the centre of the county southwest of Bodmin Moor The neighbouring parishes are Cardinham Lanhydrock Lanivet and Helland In there were people living in Bodmin In the Middle Ages Bodmin was one of the most important towns in Cornwall and the site of a monastery founded by Saint Petroc The parish church of Bodmin was built in the th century and is the second largest church in the county after Truro Cathedral
