 A symposium was a drinking party in Ancient Greece Guests were males Well bred women did not attend Boys or slave girls served the food and drink danced juggled played music or otherwise entertained the assembly These servers were usually nude The symposium was an aristocracy aristocratic institution often held to celebrate a young man s entry into adult society an athletic victory or a poetic achievement It was an occasion for male conversation and debate Party goers reclined on couches and pillows Guests played a game called kottabos Greek wrestling wrestled and held song contests A symposium is described in Plato s The Symposium The party is attended by Socrates and the young drunken and nearly nude Alcibiades
