The Ordovician is the second Period to mya It follows the Cambrian period and is followed by the Silurian period The Ordovician was named after the Welsh people Welsh tribe of the Ordovices It was defined by Charles Lapworth in He recognized that the fossils in the disputed strata were different from those of the Cambrian or the Silurian Therefore he reasoned they should be placed in a period of their own Recognition of the Ordovician period was slow in Britain but elsewhere it was quickly accepted In it was adopted as an official period of the Paleozoic era by the International Geological Congress The Ordovician ended with a series of extinction events that together amount to the second greatest extinction of the Phanerozoic This was the EndOrdovician extinction event
