The turquoise browed motmot to Costa Rica The turquoise browed motmot is a colourful bird mostly turquoise green and orange It is common and not in danger It lives in fairly open habitat s such as on the edges of forests and scrubland The motmots live in the tropical forests of Central America They are brightly coloured with a strong beak They have a signature tail with two bare feathers ending in tufts just like this bird This effect is got by the tail feathers having no barbs along the middle section This motmot has other local names Itis called Torogoz in El Salvador and Guardabarranco in Nicaragua It is the national bird of these two countries
