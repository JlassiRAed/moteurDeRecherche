 Arthur Murray was an American dance instructor and businessman whose name is most often associated with the dance studio chain which bears his name Arthur Murray was born in Galicia Austria Hungary in as Moses Teichman In August he was brought to America by his mother Sarah on the S S Friesland and landed at Ellis Island They settled in Ludlow Street in the Lower East Side of Manhattan with his father Abraham Teichmann In at the age of he taught dance at night while working as a draftsman by day He studied under the popular dance team of Vernon and Irene Castle and went to work for them Murray won his first dance contest at the Grand Central Palace a public dance hall He later became a part time dance teacher there after graduation from high school The first prize had been a silver cup but Murray went home with nothing to show for his win His partner of the evening took it it was destined for a pawnshop This loss made an impression on Murray and in later years every winner in his dance contests took home a prize
