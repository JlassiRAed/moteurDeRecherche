 Capparis decidua is commonly known as karir kair kirir karril etc It is either a small tree with many branches or a shrub of the Thar desert and other hot and dry regions in southern Asia It has a lot of thin leafless branches the small leaves being found only on young shoots It rarely grows above a height of meters The berries can be eaten and mixed with sangri form a vegetable dish called Kair sangri in Rajasthan India which is a delicacy to many
