A field gun is an artillery piece The term was first used to describe smaller gun s that could be taken with an army while marching In battle the field gun could be quickly moved about the battlefield as needed Guns installed in a fort siege cannon and mortars were too large to be moved quickly and would be used only in a long siege Napoleon used field guns with very large wheels that allowed them to be moved quickly even during a battle By moving the guns from place to place during the battle formations of enemy soldiers could be broken up to be handled by the infantry wherever they were massing dramatically increasing the overall effectiveness of the infantry
