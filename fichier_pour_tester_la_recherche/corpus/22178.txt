The Bois de Boulogne is a large public park in the west of Paris It was created between and during the reign of the Emperor Louis Napoleon It is in the th arrondissement of Paris near the suburb of Boulogne Billancourt and Neuilly sur Seine The Bois is the second largest park in Paris a bit smaller than the Bois de Vincennes on the eastern side of the city It covers an area of square kilometres and comparable in size to Richmond Park in London Within the boundaries of the Bois de Boulogne are an English landscape garden with several lakes and a cascade two smaller botanical and landscape gardens a zoo and amusement park a complex of greenhouses holding a hundred thousand plants two tracks for horse racing a tennis stadium where the French Open tournament is held each year and other attractions
