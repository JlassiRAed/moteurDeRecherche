 Casineria was a tetrapod which lived million years ago in the Mississippian It was a small animal length about centimeters It lived in what was then a fairly dry environment in what is now Scotland It had a mosaic evolution mosaic of basal amniote characters Casineria was at or very near the origin of the amniote s It may have been one of the very first true amniotes The only fossil lacks key elements most of the skull and the whole lower body is missing This makes exact analysis is difficult Casineria was an insectivore This earliest amniote had five fingers with claw s on each hand and marks the earliest known clawed foot Its name Casineria is a latin version of Cheese Bay the site near Edinburgh where it was found
