The Instrument of Surrender was a written agreement that enabled the surrender of the Pakistan Armed Forces in the Bangladesh Liberation War The surrender took place at the Ramna Race Course in Dacca on December Lieutenant General Amir Abdullah Khan Niazi A A K Niazi Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee joint commander of the Pakistan Armed Forces in East Pakistan and Lieutenant General Jagjit Singh Aurora joint commander of the Mitro Bahini Bangladesh India Allied Forces signed the instrument amid thousands of cheering crowds at the race course Air Commodore A K Khandker Deputy Commander in Chief of the Bangladesh Armed Forces and Lieutenant General J F R Jacob of the Indian Armed Forces Indian Eastern Command acted as witnesses to the surrender Subsequently around Pakistani troops and officials were taken as prisoners of war by the Indian Army the largest number of POWs since World War II They were later repatriated in under the terms of the Delhi Agreement
