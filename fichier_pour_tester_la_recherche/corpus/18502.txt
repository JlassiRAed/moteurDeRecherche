 Hatshepsut meaning Foremost of Noble Ladies Her father was Thutmose I Her predecessor was Thutmose II Her successor was Thutmose III her nephew the son of Thutmose II by a lesser wife Thutmose III was co regent with his stepmother and was head of the army but Hatshepsut was the effective ruler and the named Pharaoh Today it is generally recognized that Hatshepsut assumed the position of Pharaoh The length of her reign usually is given as twenty two years The third century B C historian Manetho gave that figure of twenty one years and nine months and he had access to many records that now are lost She died in BC which means that she became pharaoh in about BC
