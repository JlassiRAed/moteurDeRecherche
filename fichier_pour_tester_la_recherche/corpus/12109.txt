 The Coat of Arms of Belize began to be used when Belize became independent The current coat of arms is only slightly different from that used when Belize was a British colony The border is a circle of twenty five leaves Inside the circle is a Swietenia mahogany tree There are the tools of a woodcutter and a ship These show the importance of mahogany in the th and th century Belizean economy The shield is supported by two wood cutters of different races The one on the left is holding an axe The one on the right is holding an oar Again this shows the importance of the mahogany and its importance to boat building At the bottom is the national motto This means Under the Shade I Flourish The Flag of Belize has the coat of arms in its centre
