 Crows form the genus Corvus in the family Corvidae They are medium to large sized birds carnivores and scavengers The genus includes the crow the rook jay jackdaw the magpie and the large common raven The genus has or so members on all temperate continent s except for South America and some islands The Corvus makes up a third of the species in the Corvidae Crows appear to have evolved in Asia from corvid stock which had evolved in Australia The collective name for a group of crows is a flock or more poetically a murder
