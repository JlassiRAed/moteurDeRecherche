The bearded vulture also known as the lammergeier or lammergeyer is a bird of prey It is the only member of the genus Gypaetus An Old World vulture it forms a minor line of the Accipitridae with the Egyptian vulture its closest living relative They are not much more closely related to the Old World vultures proper than to hawks They differ from Old World vultures by their feathered neck It eats mainly carrion and lives and breeds on crags in high mountains in southern Europe the Caucasus Africa the Indian subcontinent and Tibet The females lay one or two eggs in mid winter that hatch at the beginning of spring Populations are resident
