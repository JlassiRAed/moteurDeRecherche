 Sinti or Sinta the area which comparative linguistics linguistic and ethnography cultural evidence indicates was the likely geographic origin of the Roma in the Southeast of what is today Pakistan While the Sinti were until quite recently chiefly nomadic today only a small percentage of the group remains unsettled In earlier times they frequently lived on the outskirts of communities generally in squalor The Sinti arrived in Germany and Austria in the Middle Ages eventually splitting into two groups Eftavagarja and the Estraxarja into Italy and Eastern Europe mainly what are now Croatia Hungary Transylvania the Czech Republic and Slovakia eventually taking various regional names
