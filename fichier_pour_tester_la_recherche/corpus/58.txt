Sikandar Lodi was the sultan of Delhi and second ruler of the Lodi dynasty after Bahlul Lodi He was the most successful sultan of the Lodi dynasty of the Delhi Sultanate Sikandar Lodi ruled the sultanate from July to November Sikandar Lodi was born to the first sultan of the Lodi dynasty Bahlul Lodi and Bibi Ambha daughter of a Hindu goldsmith After he became the ruler he proved that he is an ablest ruler He encouraged trade across his territories but discriminated Hindus He conquered Gwalior and Bihar regions Sikandar Lodi constructed the present day city of Agra He wrote Persian poetry using the pen name Gulrukhi Sikandar enlarged his territories that extended from the region of Punjab to Bengal and the territories between River Satluj and Bundelkhand region He died in November and was buried in the Lodi Gardens
