 Richmond Park is a hectare urban park near central London UK It is the largest of the eight Royal Parks of London royal parks in London and is famous for Red Deer Red and Fallow Deer Notable features in the park include Pembroke Lodge once the home of Prime Minister of the United Kingdom the British Prime Minister John Russell st Earl Russell Lord John Russell Also in the park is White Lodge home of the Royal Ballet School and once a royal residence One house in the park Thatched House Lodge the residence of Princess Alexandra the Honourable Lady Ogilvy Princess Alexandra The park is fully open to the public with access by road bicycles and pedestrians It is a parliamentary constituency comprising the electoral wards of Barnes East Sheen Ham Petersham Kew Mortlake North Richmond and South Richmond in the London Borough of Richmond upon Thames It also covers the wards of Canbury Coombe Hill Coombe Vale and Tudor in the Royal Borough of Kingston upon Thames The present Member of Parliament MP is Zac Goldsmith
