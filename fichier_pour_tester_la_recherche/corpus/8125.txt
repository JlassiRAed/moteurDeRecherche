 Woodstock Palace was a royal residence in the English town of Woodstock Oxfordshire The title of palace was first used to refer to it during the twelfth century when it was favoured by King Henry I of England In about he created a zoo in the grounds His grandson Henry II of England Henry II was also fond of Woodstock and spent time here with his mistress Rosamund Clifford Woodstock Palace was destroyed during the English Civil War
