 Hiroshima is a magazine article written by Pulitzer Prize winner John Hersey that appeared in The New Yorker in August one year after the United States dropped an atomic bomb on the city of Hiroshima Japan The article was soon made into a book It described how Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki the bombing affected the lives of six individuals Reverend Mr Kiyoshi Tanimoto a Methodism Methodist minister educated in the United States at Emory University was yards from the center of the explosion Mrs Hatsuyo Nakamura a war widow and seamstress the mother of three young children was yards from the center of the explosion Dr Masakazu Fujii a prosperous doctor and owner of a private hospital was yards from the center of the explosion Father Wilhelm Kleinsorge a Jesuit priest stationed in the city was yards from the center of the explosion Dr Terufumi Sasaki a young doctor at the Red Cross hospital was yards from the center of the explosion and Miss Toshiko Sasaki was yards from the center of the explosion Each account is followed by a brief statement describing how close each person was to the center of the blast
