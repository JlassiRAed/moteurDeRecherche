 Swati is part of the Nguni group of Bantu languages It is spoken by about million people in Swaziland and South Africa It is one of the eleven official languages in South Africa It is taught in the schools of Swaziland and in some South African schools Swati is closely related to Phuthi a language spoken in Lesotho and to Zulu Ndebele and Xhosa Swati has four main varietes Shiselweni Hhohho Manzini and Shiselweni They correspond with the four administrative regions of Swaziland
