The Bundestag is the legislature of Germany It meets in Berlin in the Reichstag building which was specially rebuilt ready for the Bundestag to move from Bonn The Bundestag has at least members Every years the German people vote A party gets seats when it gets at least of the votes Every voter has two votes one for a person and one for a party members each represent a constituency just as in the British House of Commons or the US House of Representatives The other half are elected from a party list in each States of Germany Land The total number of seats a party gets depends on the number of the second party votes it gets But if a party wins more constituency seats than its share of the party votes then it keeps those extra seats These extra seats or extra mandates are the reason the Bundestag sometimes has more than members If a party gets extra mandates the other parties get some extra seats too to make sure every party gets exactly its share of party seats This system of giving extra mandates was changed in Before if a party won extra mandates then it got to put more members in the Bundestag which gave the party more influence over the Bundestag than it won in the elections The Bundestag president is named Norbert Lammert He oversees the sessions of the body
