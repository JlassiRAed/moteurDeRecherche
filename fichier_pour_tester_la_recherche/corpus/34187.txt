 Muhammad Ahmad bin Abd Allah was a religious leader of the Sufi Samaniyya order in Sudan On June he proclaimed himself as the Mahdi or messianic redeemer of the Islam ic faith His proclamation came during a period of widespread resentment among the Arabic part of the Sudanese population They resented the oppressive policies of their Turco Egyptian rulers There were Messiah messianic beliefs among the various Sudanese religious sects of the time There were earlier Mahdist movements in West Africa and Wahabism and other puritanical forms of Islamic revivalism The root cause of this was the growing military and economic dominance of the Europe an powers throughout the th century The British influenced the Sudan and had claimed it as a Joint Anglo Egyptian Condominion In general the British were behind the rule of the Muhammed Ali dynasty in Egypt From his announcement of the Mahdiyya in June until the fall of Khartoum in January Muhammad Ahmad led a successful military campaign against the Turco Egyptian government of the Sudan The fighting ended with the death of Gordon in Khartoum After Muhammad Ahmad s unexpected death on June only six months after the conquest of Khartoum his chief deputy took over the administration of the Sudan
