 Spahn Ranch also known as the Spahn Movie Ranch was a large movie ranch in Los Angeles County California It was used for filming mostly old Western movie s and television program s With its mountain s and western scenery the ranch was ideal for Western cowboy and Indian type shows Some of the well known TV shows which were made at Spahn were episodes of Bonanza The Lone Ranger and Zorro The ranch is no longer in use The entrance was at Santa Susana Pass Road but the street number has changed The ranch was very popular due to its being a home for Charles Manson and his followers They lived there during and It was owned and named after a dairy farmer named George Spahn
