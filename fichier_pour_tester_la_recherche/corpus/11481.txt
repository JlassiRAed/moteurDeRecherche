The Bodleian Library Copyright and Related Rights Act It has had rights of legal deposit for books since the th century Though members of the University may borrow some books from some dependent libraries the vast majority of the material held by the Bodleian may only be read within the library buildings Besides books periodicals and manuscripts there are smaller collections of other types of material The Library occupies a group of five buildings near Broad Street in central Oxford the oldest is the late medieval Duke Humfrey s Library and the newest the New Bodleian of the s Since the th century a number of underground stores have been built below parts of these Though Duke Humfrey s Library was a library for the university from to the Bodleian itself was founded by Sir Thomas Bodley in the years to In the following centuries it greatly increased in size through collections of books donated by generous benefactors and through books received through legal deposit Many notable scholars have been heads of the library the present Bodley s Librarian is Sarah Thomas
