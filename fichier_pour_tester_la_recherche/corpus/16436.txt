The Greek shield of Ancient Greece was called a hoplon or aspis It was from this word that hoplite is derived A hoplon shield was a deeply dished shield made of wood Some shields had a thin sheet of bronze on the outer face This was placed just around the rim In some periods the shield was decorated At other times it remained undecorated These large shields were designed for a mass of hoplites to push forward into the opposing army and it was their most essential equipment Probably the most famous decoration is the Sparta Spartan This was a capital lambda The shields of Thebes Theban hoplite s were sometimes decorated with a sphinx or the club of Herakles
