 Acipenseriformes Polypteriformes The Chondrostei are a sub class of ray finned fish the Actinopterygii They include the sturgeon s huge fish which provide caviar The Chondrostei are mainly cartilage cartilaginous fish with some bone ossification There are species divided among two orders The orders are the Acipenseriformes This group has at times been classified with the shark s the similarities are obvious not only do the chondrosteans mostly lack bone the structure of the jaw is more akin to that of sharks than other bony fish Both lack Scale However the fossil record suggests that these fish have more in common with the Teleostei than their external appearance might suggest
