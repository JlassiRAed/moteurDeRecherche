The vibraslap is a percussion instrument It is a modern version of the Jawbone instrument jawbone Due to the fragile nature of the jawbone the vibra slap was created to provide a stronger alternative with a similar sound It has been used mainly in popular music Several Rock and roll rock bands have used this sound for the last decades of the th century and into the st century It is also popular in Latin music The vibraslap is made of a cowbell shaped hollow box and a wooden ball connected by a steel rod The steel rod is bent in an L shape that allows the performer to hold the rod in one hand and strike the ball with the palm of their other hand The steel rod acts as a sort of spring that vibration vibrates the box on the other end The box acts as a resonating body for a metal mechanism placed inside with a number of loosely fastened pins or rivet s that vibrate and rattle against the box much like the teeth of the jawbone The vibraslap is produced in a number of sizes using different materials such as wood metal or composite materials The Vibra Slap was the first patent granted to the instrument manufacturing company Latin Percussion The Vibra Slap s inventor was Martin Cohen It is sometimes sold as a Donkey Call or Rattleslap
