 The Rame Peninsula is a peninsula in southeast Cornwall England in the United Kingdom The peninsula is surrounded by the English Channel to the south Plymouth Sound is to the east The estuary of the River Lynher lies to the north The largest Human settlement settlement is Torpoint It is on the eastern coast facing Devonport Devon Devonport in Plymouth Devon The peninsula is named after Rame Head to the south of the peninsula The peninsula also includes the village and parish of Rame Maker with Rame Rame The whole area of the peninsula is designated an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty
