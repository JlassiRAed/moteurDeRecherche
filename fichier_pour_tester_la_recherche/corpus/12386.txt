 Mount Buninyong is large extinct volcano kilometres south east of Ballarat Victoria Australia The scoria cone of the volcano is more than metres higher than the land around it which makes it one of biggest scoria volcanoes in Victoria There is a road that goes up to the top of the mountain and there are a number of communication towers and a bushfire watching tower There are three walking tracks the Crater Walk the Summit Walk and the Southern Walk The name Buninyong comes from the Australian Aborigine Australian Aboriginal people known as the Wauthaurung Their word for the mountain was Bunnin Yowang which meant big hill like knee
