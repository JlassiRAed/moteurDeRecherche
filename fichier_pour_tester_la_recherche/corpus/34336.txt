The pronghorn family Antilocapridae It is a small ruminant mammal which looks a bit like an antelope The pronghorn lives in North America It lives in the prairie s but sometimes also in the desert and the Rocky Mountains The antilocaprids evolved in North America where they filled a niche similar to that of the bovids that evolved in the Old World During the Miocene and Pliocene they were a diverse and successful group with many different species Some had horns with bizarre shapes or had four or even six horns
