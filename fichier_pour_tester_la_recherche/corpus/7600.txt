The Kori Creek strip of water disputed between the Republic of India and Pakistan claimed by India as a part of the Indian Rann of Kutch marshlands The creek which opens up into the Arabian Sea divides the Kutch region of the Indian state of Gujarat with the Sindh province of Pakistan The long standing dispute hinges in the actual demarcation from the mouth of Kori Creek to the top of Kori Creek and from the top of Kori Creek eastward to a point on the line designated on the Western Terminus From this point onwards the boundary is unambiguously fixed as defined by the Tribunal Award of The creek itself is in the uninhabited marshlands During the monsoon season between June and September the creek floods its banks and envelops the low lying salty mudflats around it During the winter season the area is home to flamingoes and other migratory birds
