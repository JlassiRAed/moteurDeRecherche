 Leptospiras are long thin motile spirochaete s They may be free living or parasitic They survive well in fresh water soil and mud in tropical areas They have been studied at the CDC Molecular taxonomic studies at CDC and elsewhere have identified named and unnamed species of pathogenic leptospires Leptospirosis causes a wide range of symptoms and some infected persons may have no symptoms at all Symptoms of leptospirosis include high fever severe headache chills muscle aches and vomiting and may include jaundice red eyes abdominal pain diarrhea or a rash
