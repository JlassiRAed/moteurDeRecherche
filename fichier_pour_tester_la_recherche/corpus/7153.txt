 The Sri Maha Bodhi is a Sacred Fig tree in Anuradhapura Sri Lanka It is said to be a tree grown from a seed from the famous Bodhi tree under which the first Gautama Buddha Buddha became enlightened It was planted in BC and is the oldest living human planted tree in the world with a known planting date It was planted on a high terrace about above the ground and surrounded by railings and today it is one of the most sacred objects of the Buddhism Buddhists in Sri Lanka and respected by Buddhists all over the world This wall was built during the reign of King Kirthi Sri Rajasingha to protect it from wild elephant s which might have attacked the tree
