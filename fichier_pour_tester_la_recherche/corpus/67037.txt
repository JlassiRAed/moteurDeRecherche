The Second Great Awakening was a religious movement in the United States in the early and mid s It was led by people such as Charles Grandison Finney Henry Ward Beecher Lyman Beecher E Edward Everett dward Everett and Joseph Smith It started in upstate New York but spread to New England and the Midwest One of these was the Temperance Movement which believed that drinking alcohol was not good for society The other was abolition which wanted to end slavery People such as Harriet Beecher Stowe and William Lloyd Garrison wrote books and newspapers about how slavery should stop They also formed political movements which included the Liberty Party the Free Soil Party and the Republican Party
