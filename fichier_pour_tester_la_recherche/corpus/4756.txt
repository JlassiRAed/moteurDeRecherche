 Gaul was the name given in ancient times to the area of Western Europe that included northern Italy France Belgium western Switzerland and the parts of the Netherlands and Germany on the west bank of the Rhine river Many Celt ic tribes lived in the region and were known by the Romans simply as Gauls In English the word Gaul may also mean a Celt ic inhabitant of that region although the expression may be used for all ancient speakers of the Gaulish language as well In this way Gaul and Celt are sometimes used in the same sense Gauls under Brennus
