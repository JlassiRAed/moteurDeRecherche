The Neue Wache is a building in central Berlin the capital of Germany It is located on the north side of the Unter den Linden The Neue Wache was designed by the architect Karl Friedrich Schinkel was built in and is a leading example of German neoclassicism Originally it was built as a guardhouse for the troops of the Crown Prince of Prussia the building has been used as a war memorial since King Frederick William III of Prussia ordered the construction of the Neue Wache as a guard house for the nearby Palace of the Crown Prince to replace the old Artillery Guard House He commissioned Schinkel the leading exponent of neoclassicism in architecture to design the building this was Schinkel s first major commission in Berlin
