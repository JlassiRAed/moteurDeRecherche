A county borough was a type of local government district in England until abolished in Local government powers were divided between county council s and urban district urban or rural district s For example rural districts may not be able to maintain footpaths or street lighting because of their size Urban districts were based in a town and were able handle these jobs Schools were the responsibility of county councils because districts were too small to run all types of schools themselves A county borough was a town big enough to run all its own local government so the local county council had no power inside the county borough
