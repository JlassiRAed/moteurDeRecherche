 Appendages are parts of animals attached the main body Many arthropod s have appendages on each or most of their segment s Millipede s for example have many segments and almost all carry two legs Insect s have exactly six legs two on each segment of the thorax Antenna attached to their heads are another type of appendage Wings parts of the tail legs some mouthparts external sex organ s can all be described as appendages Appendages may be uniramous as in insects and centipede s where each appendage comprises a single series of segments or it may be biramous as in many crustacea ns where each appendage branches into two sections All arthropod appendages are variations of the same basic structure with modified appendages such as legs instead of antennae
