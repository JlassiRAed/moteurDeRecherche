A dissenting opinion is an Legal opinion opinion in a legal case It is written by one or more judge s expressing disagreement with the majority opinion of the court When not necessarily referring to a legal decision this can also be referred to as a minority report Dissenting opinions are normally written at the same time as the majority opinion and any concurring opinion s They are also delivered and publishing published at the same time A dissenting opinion does not create binding precedent nor does it become a part of case law However they can sometimes be cited as a form of persuasive authority in subsequent cases when arguing that the court s Holding holding should be limited or overturned In some cases a previous dissent is used to spur a change in the law A later case may result in a majority opinion adopting a particular rule of law formerly advocated in dissent As with concurring opinions the difference in opinion between dissents and majority opinions can often highlight the precise holding of the majority opinion Chief Justice of the United States Chief Justice Charles Evans Hughes stated in A dissent in a Court of last resort is an appeal The dissent may disagree with the majority for any number of reasons a different interpretation of the existing case law the application of different principles or a different Judicial interpretation interpretation of the facts
