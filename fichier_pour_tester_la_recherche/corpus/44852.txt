A borough is an administrative division in many countries It is a kind of local government The term borough can means a self governing township In medieval times boroughs were Human settlement settlements with some self government Boroughs were particularly common in England Germany and Scotland In medieval England boroughs had also the right to elect members of parliament Usually a borough is a single town with its own local government But in some cities it is a sub division of the city In such cases the borough will normally have either limited powers delegated to it by the city s local government or no powers at all At certain times London has had no overall city government and London boroughs were the main unit of local government for Londoners In other places such as Alaska a borough does not mean a single township but a whole region In Australia borough can mean a town and its surrounding area e g the Borough of Victoria Queenscliff e Boroughs exist in United Kingdom more specifically in England and Northern Ireland in the Canada Canadian Provinces and territories of Canada province of Quebec in some U S state states of the United States in Israel and formerly they also existed in New Zealand At the end of a word borough is more common in Scotland and the American South and West
