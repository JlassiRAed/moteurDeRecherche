Indian Wars is the name generally used in the United States to describe a series of conflicts between the colonial or federal government and the native people of North America These wars which ranged from the th century assimilation or Indian removal forced relocation to Indian reservation s The Indian Wars comprised a series of smaller wars American Indians who were diverse peoples with their own distinct tribal histories were no more a single people than the Europeans They lived in societies organized in a variety of ways American Indians usually made decisions about war and peace at the local level They sometimes fought as part of formal alliances One example is the Iroquois Iroquois Confederation They also sometimes used temporary confederacies inspired by leaders such as Tecumseh
