The numbat is a marsupial from open woodland s in western Australia It is also called the banded anteater because it eats termites It is unusual in being one of the few diurnal marsupials It has no pouch but the mother carries round her four young on her stomach At night they take shelter in hollow logs The numbat has a life span of years under human care These solitary long tailed termite eaters are in danger of extinction very few remain in the wild When Europeans arrived the numbat was found across the New South Wales and Victoria Victorian borders west to the Indian Ocean and as far north as the southwest corner of the Northern Territory It was at home in a wide range of woodland and semi arid habitats The deliberate release of the European red fox in the th century wiped out the entire numbat population in Victoria NSW South Australia and the Northern Territory and almost all numbats in Western Australia as well By the late s the population was well under individuals concentrated in two small areas not far from Perth Western Australia Perth Dryandra and Perup The numbat is the emblem of Western Australia
