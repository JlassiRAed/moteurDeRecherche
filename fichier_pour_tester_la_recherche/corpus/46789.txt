An overture is a piece of music for the orchestra to play at the beginning of an opera or ballet The word comes from the French word for opening because it opens the show Overtures usually have tunes which are going to be heard during the opera or ballet In this way it prepares the audience for what is to come Many overtures in the th century were simply background music to get the audience s attention used the same overture again and again for his next operas or just changed bits of it Composers like Christoph Willibald Gluck and later Richard Wagner Not all composers wrote overtures to their operas Giuseppe Verdi often go straight into the first act or they just have a very short prelude In the th century many Romantic music Romantic composers wrote concert overtures These pieces did not belong to any opera or ballet they were just written to be heard at concerts They often had a descriptive title because they told some sort of story e g Felix Mendelssohn so they were called tone poem tone poems Overtures are usually played in sonata form
