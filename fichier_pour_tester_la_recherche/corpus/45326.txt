 Shore crabs are a common crustacean They are often found around the sea shore They come in a variety of colours They are often green or brown They have eight legs and two large pincers The width across the shell is up to They are normally found in the North Atlantic or the Baltic Sea but are beginning to live in many other areas of the world Shore crabs live on beach es and rocky shores Shore crabs are a nocturnal predator which means it hunts at night They eat small crustaceans molluscs worms and some fish A female shore crab can lay up to nearly thousand eggs at once They carry the eggs with them until they hatch Like most crabs when a shore crab gets too big for its shell it moulting sheds its shell After that it has a new soft shell which soon hardens
