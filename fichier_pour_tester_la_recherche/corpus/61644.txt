A feud This is called guilt by association Feuds start because one party thinks they were attacked insulted or otherwise harmed by the other party Intense feelings of resentment trigger the initial revenge which causes the other party to feel the same way The dispute is then fueled by a long running cycle of retaliatory violence This ongoing cycle of provocation and retaliation makes it very difficult to end the feud peacefully Feuds frequently involve the original parties family members and or associates They can last for generation s Up to the early modern period feuds were considered legitimate legal instruments The state or ruler even made laws for certain aspects of feuds Once modern Centralization centralizing states asserted and enforced a Monopoly on the legitimate use of physical force monopoly on legitimate use of force feuds became illegal and the concept acquired its current negative connotation
