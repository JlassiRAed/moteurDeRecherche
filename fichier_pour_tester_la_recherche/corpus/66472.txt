The Inline four engine or Straight four is an internal combustion engine with all four Cylinder cylinder s All four cylinders are mounted in a straight line along one crankshaft It can be powered by different types of fuels including gasoline Diesel fuel diesel and natural gas The single bank of cylinders may be in a vertical direction L is often used to avoid confusion between the digit and the letter I The inline four layout is a mechanically simple engine It has a natural basic engine balance It is smoother than one two and three cylinder engines This makes it popular for economy cars It does have a problem with secondary engine balance This causes minor vibrations in smaller engines These vibrations become worse as engine size and power increase The more powerful engines used in larger cars use different an engine layout to avoid this problem
