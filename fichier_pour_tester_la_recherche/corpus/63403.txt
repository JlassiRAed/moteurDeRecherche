The Dunkirk evacuation sometimes called Operation Dynamo or The Miracle of Dunkirk was a British mission to rescue Allies of World War II Allied soldiers from the beach es of Dunkirk France from May to June Over Allied soldiers were trapped in Dunkirk by the German army after the Battle of Dunkirk For some reason the German commander ordered a halt to the surrounded city Winston Churchill then ordered any ship or boat available to head for Dunkirk to pick up the soldiers Over ships participated in the evacuation consisting mostly of fishing and pleasure boats although there were some larger ships also taking part Originally their intention was to pick up British soldiers and it was anticipated the Germans would try to stop them On the first day only soldiers were picked up from the harbour but none from the beach On the second day soldiers were evacuated including some from the beaches On the third day soldiers were successfully rescued followed by on day four including the first French soldiers Evacuation reached its peak on days five and six when over Allied soldiers escaped By day seven nearly all the British had left but the boats continued to pick up soldiers a day Evacuation ended after nine days by which time British soldiers and French soldiers had escaped to Great Britain
