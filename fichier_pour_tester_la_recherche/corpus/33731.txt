 Snowy River National Park is a national park in Victoria km east of Melbourne Australia Melbourne The park includes much of the Snowy River A lot of the park is a protected wilderness area with no access for vehicles The park in the Little River Gorge area has one of the last natural habitats for the endangered species endangered Brush tailed Rock Wallaby Numbers for this species are estimated as extremely small with the rugged terrain making it difficult to count the population Over species have been found in the park are rare or threatened species threatened in Victoria These include the Long footed Potoroo Spotted Quoll Giant Burrowing Frog and Cyclodomorphus michaeli Eastern She oak Skink
