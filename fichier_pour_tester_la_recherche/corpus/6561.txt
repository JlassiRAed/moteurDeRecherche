 Dsungaripterus It had an unusual bony crest running along its snout and long narrow curved jaws with a pointed tip The crest may have acted as a rudder when flying or may have been a secondary sex characteristic Dsungaripterus s head and neck were together almost a metre long Its most notable feature are its long narrow upcurved jaws with a pointed tip making the animal look like a pair of flying tweezers It had no teeth in the front part of its jaws which were probably used to remove shellfish and worms from cracks in rocks or from sandy muddy beaches It had knobbly flat teeth more to the back of the jaw that were well suited for crushing shellfish
