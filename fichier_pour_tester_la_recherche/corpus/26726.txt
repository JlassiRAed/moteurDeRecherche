 Bamboo is a name for over species of giant grass es in different genus genera All bamboos have wood like stems Bamboo mainly grows in Africa Americas America and in Asia but can easily grow in Europe Bamboo grows in clumps a day Almost all species of bamboo have hollow stems divided into node s or joints The stem can be up to cm in diameter Each of the node has one side Budding bud Not all of those buds develop into branches but some do This makes bamboo one of the few grasses that have a branch structure Bamboo rarely flowers Some species only flower once and then die off The distance of two joints in a bamboo is the basis of a traditional Japanese unit of measurement shaku
