The Egerton Gospel is a set of papurus papyri that are now in the British Museum The papyri have been dated to the nd century second century The text they contain may have been written AD It is one of the oldest known fragments of any gospel or any codex Soon after the British Museum acquired it in the summer of it was printed in It is also called the Unknown Gospel as no ancient source makes reference to it in addition to being entirely unknown before its publication The surviving fragments include four stories a controversy similar to John and curing a leper similar to Matt Mark Luke and Luke a controversy about paying tribute to Caesar analogous to Matt Mark Luke an incomplete account of a miracle on the Jordan River bank perhaps carried out to illustrate the parable about seeds growing miraculously The last story has no equivalent in canonical Gospels
