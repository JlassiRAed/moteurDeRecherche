A crystal radio sometimes called a crystal set is a very simple Amplitude modulation AM radio receiver that does not need battery batteries to work A long wire antenna gathers enough of the radio signal s from nearby transmitter s to power a crystal diode which Crystal radios have come and gone many times in history They were built in the early days of radio broadcasting by amateur s and people who could not afford a well made radio They became popular again during the Great Depression when money was scarce During World War II United States Army American soldier s built radios that used a razor blade and pencil point instead of a crystal After the war ended amateurs built more crystal sets and children began to make them as science and hobby projects Later during the Vietnam War prisoner of war prisoners of war again built simple radios and soldiers used Slinky springs as antennas Hobby clubs still exist that give information to persons who want to build a crystal radio or similar radio project
