In the past the Rhinelands meant an area on both banks of the Rhine in central Europe But now the Rhineland has become a general word for areas of Germany along the middle and lower Rhine It borders Luxembourg Belgium and the Netherlands to the west and the Rhine to the east After the French were defeated in the early th century the German and Dutch speaking areas at the middle and lower Rhine were added to the Kingdom of Prussia The area was called Rhine Province After World War I of the early th century the western part of Rhineland was under Entente control and demilitarized under the Treaty of Versailles German forces sent armies back to it in three years before World War II
