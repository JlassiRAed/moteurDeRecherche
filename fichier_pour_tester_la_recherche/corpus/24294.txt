The Violin Concerto in D major Op is a work for solo violin and orchestra It was written by Ludwig van Beethoven in for Franz Clement first violinist and conductor of the Theatre an der Wien orchestra It met with lukewarm approval It did not receive its rightful due until when it was conducted in London by Mendelssohn The concerto opens with a few strokes on the timpani that led some music lovers to call the work the Kettledrum Concerto The first movement lasts about minutes The second movement is serene and free from the dramatic turbulence of the first The third movement displays a happy liveliness and makes great demands upon the soloist s skills
