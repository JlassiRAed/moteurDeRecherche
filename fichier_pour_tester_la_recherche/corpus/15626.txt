The Fiordland penguin Eudyptes pachyrhynchus is a crested penguin They have white stripes on their belly cheeks and they have a black back They have bright yellow eyebrows and when they get older they turn darker They also have an orange beak big feet and they have small black tails They weigh pounds and are inches tall They eat fish squid and krill is seals Most Fiordland penguins live in the woodland on the coast of south New Zealand but some also live in Tasmania They live up to to years in the wild They swim very fast in the water near New Zealand
