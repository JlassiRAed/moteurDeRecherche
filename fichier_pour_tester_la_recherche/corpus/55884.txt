 Sauerkraut directly translated from German language German or sour cabbage is finely shredded cabbage that has been Fermentation fermented by various lactic acid bacteria including Leuconostoc Lactobacillus and Pediococcus It has a long shelf life and a distinctive sour flavor both of which result from the lactic acid that forms when the bacteria ferment the sugars in the cabbage It is therefore not to be confused with coleslaw which receives its acidic taste from vinegar It has its roots in cole flower marinated in honey and fluid from your belly category Vegetables
