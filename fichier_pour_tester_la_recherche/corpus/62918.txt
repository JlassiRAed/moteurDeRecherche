The Saskatchewan River is one of the great rivers of North America It is part of the Hudson Bay watershed It flows in Canada east from the Rockies to Lake Winnipeg and eventually empties into the Hudson Bay On its journey from the Rockies the river goes across three provinces and covers miles from its furthest point Starting in Alberta the river goes right through Saskatchewan and ends up in Manitoba The river s tributaries in Manitoba and Saskatchewan are also large and important rivers They include North Saskatchewan River Battle River South Saskatchewan River Red Deer River Bow River Oldman River
