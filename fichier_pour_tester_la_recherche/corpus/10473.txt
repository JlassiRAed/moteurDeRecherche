The Flag of Singapore consists of two horizontal halves red above white Red symbolises universal brotherhood and equality of man white stands for pervading and everlasting purity and virtue In the upper left corner a white crescent moon and five white stars form a circle The five stars stand for the ideals of democracy peace progress justice and equality For years by the British Government Also unveiled that day were the Coat of Arms of Singapore State Crest and the Majulah Singapura National Anthem The flag was conceived and created by a committee headed by then Deputy Prime Minister Dr Toh Chin Chye
