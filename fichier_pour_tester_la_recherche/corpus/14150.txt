The origin of the blues Little is known about the origin of the music known as the blues One early mention comes from when an archaeologist in Mississippi described the songs of African American black workers which had lyrical themes and technical elements in common with the blues There are few characteristics common to all blues The genre takes its shape from the peculiarities of each individual performance Many of these blues elements such as the call and response format can be traced back to the music of Africa When African slaves went to work in North America they took musical traditions with them Early forms of music include spirituals and work songs Spirituals were religious songs and work songs were sung to the rhythm of working Work songs included a vocal call and response or where the lead drummer makes a beat and then the others respond to it The majority of blues songs have four beats in a bar and are built on the bar blues form which uses three phrases each four bars long They can also use the blue scale scale
