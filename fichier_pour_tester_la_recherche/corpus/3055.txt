In music a melisma is the technique of singing one syllable to a lot of notes They are often called vocal runs or simply runs Music which uses melismas can be called melismatic In Western music melismas are often used in opera s or any other music which is written for the singer to show off the voice Melismatic music is the opposite of music where each syllable has its own note In the Alleluia Chorus from Handel Handel s Messiah is set to a run of fast notes This example can also be described as word painting Handel uses the melisma to make it sound like someone shaking something Melismas are used a lot in music from many different cultures
