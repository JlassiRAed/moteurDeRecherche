 The Sand dollars are species of flattened burrowing sea urchin s belonging to the Order order Clypeasteroida Echinarachnius parma the Common Sand Dollar is widespread in the Northern Hemisphere from the intertidal zone to considerable depths Sand dollars can be found in temperate and tropical zones but not in Great Britain All sand dollars have a rigid skeleton known as a test This is the typical white disk found washed up on beaches The living animals have a skin of movable spines on the test Movement is done by the action of the spines Like other sea urchin s sand dollars have five paired rows of pore s The pores are arranged in a petal like pattern These pores are perforations in the endoskeleton through which the tube feet used in gas exchange project from the body
