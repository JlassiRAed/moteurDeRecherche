 Monocytes are a type of white blood cell part of the human body s immune system They are usually identified in stained smears by their large two lobed Cell nucleus nucleus They are a kind of reserve cell which turn into macrophage s and immune helper cells called dendritic cell s Monocytes work at two speeds in the immune system To gradually replenish resident macrophage s and dendritic cells under normal conditions and To move quickly to infected tissue in response to inflammation signals There they divide and differentiate into macrophage s and dendritic cells to cause an immune response Half of all monocytes are stored as a reserve in the spleen the rest are circulating or in tissues
