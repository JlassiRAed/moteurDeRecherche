 Meju is a brick of fermented soybean soybeans in Korean cuisine It is used in Korean cuisine to make essential condiment condiments such as soy sauce There are several ways to make a brick of soybeans Traditionally boiled soybeans were crushed in a mortar and made into a brick shape to be tied up with straw Meju was commonly made in any Korean home in the past Condiments made with mejus all tasted different because the production of meju was not standardized As many Koreans now buy Korean traditional condiments instead of making them it is difficult to find the wide variety of unique tasting condiments made from homemade meju
