 Investiture means the formal installation of somebody in public office by formally giving him the insignia The term is normally reserved for formal offices of state aristocracy and church In the Middle Ages investiture was the Ceremony ceremonial transfer of a fief by an overlord to a vassal The lord invested the vassal with a fiefdom by giving a symbol of the land or office From feudal times up to the present the term has been used in canon law to refer to a cleric receiving the symbols of spiritual office such as the pastoral ring mitre and Staff of office staff signifying transfer of the office
