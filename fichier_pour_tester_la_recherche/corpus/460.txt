A Fibula is an ancient brooch Technically the latin term fibulae meant Ancient Rome Roman brooches but the term is widely used for brooches from the entire Ancient history ancient and Middle Ages early medieval world Unlike modern brooches fibulae were not only decorative they originally served a practical function to fasten clothes including cloak s Fibulae replaced straight pin s that were used to fasten clothing in the Neolithic period and Bronze Age In turn fibulae were replaced as clothing fasteners in the Middle Ages by button s Their descendent the modern safety pin remains in use today There are hundreds of different types and variations of fibulae They are usually divided into families or groups based on historical period geography and or cultural grouping Fibulae are also divided into classes based on their general forms
