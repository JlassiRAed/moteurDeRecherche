The phonograph also called the record player or gramophone is an Electronics electronic device that plays recorded sound It was the most common device for playing recorded music from the s through the s It was invented by Thomas Edison Early phonographs recorded sound on cylinder s in addition to playing sound The phonograph plays sounds from a vinyl record The record is placed onto the turntable The turntable spins the record while a lever with a small needle on the bottom scrapes in between the little grooves in the vinyl When this happens music is played These modern machines do not record They play records that were made in a factory
