 Jelly fungi are a paraphyletic group of several Fungi fungal orders from different classes of the subphylum Agaricomycotina The name comes from their form or growth habit Their mushroom fruiting body is foliose or irregularly branched It looks like jelly Actually many are somewhat rubbery and gelatinous When dried jelly fungi become hard and shriveled when exposed to water they return to their original form Some jelly fungi can be eaten raw poisonous jelly fungi are rare However many species have an unpalatable texture or taste However some species Snow fungus Tremella fuciformis for example are not only edible but prized for use in soup and vegetable dishes
