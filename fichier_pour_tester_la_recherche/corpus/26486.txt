 Avro Lancaster was developed out of Avro Avro Manchester It was the most used RAF bomber in the Second World War The changes made on Avro like four Rolls Royce Merlin X instead of two powerplants and longer wings have led to the new name Avro Royal Air Force ordered pieces of this airplane After the success on the war places Avro sold the production licence to other companies One of those was Packard who bought the licence for powerplant production and improved them The variant with the most produced pieces was Avro B I a plane which was the base of RAF Bomber Command at that time
