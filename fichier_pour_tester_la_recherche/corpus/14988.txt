The Synod of Dort was a national council that took place in in the town of Dordrecht in the Netherlands The Dutch Reformed Church held the meeting in order to discuss a very important issue in the Dutch churches that begin with the teachings of Jacobus Arminius and the rise of Arminianism The first meeting was on November and the last meeting which was the th took place on May Members from the Reformed churches in eight different countries were also invited to this meeting The name Dort is the English name for the Dutch town of Dordrecht and is still used as the pronunciation of the name today This synod or meeting is usually called the Synod of Dordt or the Synod of Dordrecht
