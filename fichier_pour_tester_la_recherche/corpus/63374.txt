 Hangul or Hangeul It was made by Sejong the Great the th King of the Joseon Joseon Dynasty in and it is still used by many people When Hangeul was spread out King Sejong published Hunminjeong eum Haeryebon which was chosen as the Memory of the World by UNESCO in In this book the design ideas of Hangeul and information on its usage were written down in detail The book Hunminjeong eum Haeryebon states the design ideas of Hangeul The first one is that the consonants of Hangeul look like vocal organs The second one is that the vowels of Hangeul are based on Cheonji in Because of these ideas Hangeul is well known for being easy to learn Although it was only used by the lower classes at first Hangeul became the official writing script of Korea in the late th century Today it is the most used writing system of both North and South Korea North Korea abolished the use of Hanja and today North Koreans write only in Hangeul In South Korea people write mostly in Hangeul although in some contexts Hanja is still used
