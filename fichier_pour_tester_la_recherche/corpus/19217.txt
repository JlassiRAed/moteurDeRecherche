 The national flag of Oman with a red bar on the left that contains the national emblem of Oman The white stands for peace and prosperity the green for fertility and the Green Mountains and the red for battles against foreign invaders The red is also the colour of the former flag of the nation when it was known simply as the Muscat and Oman Sultanate of Musca and Oman Until Oman used the plain red banner of the indigenous people the Kharijite Muslims In the Sultan introduced a complete new set of national flags Bands of green and white were added to the fly and the national emblem the badge of the Albusaidi Dynasty was placed in the canton This shows crossed swords over a Gambia a traditional curved dagger White has been associated historically with the Imam the religious leader of Oman and at times the political rival to the ruling Sultan It also symbolizes peace Green is traditionally associated with the Jebel al Akhdar or Green Mountains which lie toward the north of the country Red is a common color in Gulf state flags The national emblem is said to date back to the th century A curved dagger is fastened over a pair of crossed swords An ornate horsebit links the weapons
