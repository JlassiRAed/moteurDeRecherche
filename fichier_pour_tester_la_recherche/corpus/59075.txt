The Italian Peninsula The peninsula is about long starting from the Po Po Valley in the north The Apennine Mountains cover most of its length It has mainly a Mediterranean climate though in the mountainous parts the climate is much cooler Since the reign of Roman Emperor Augustus the northern border of the peninsula has been set on the Alps drainage basin Geographically however its northern end runs from the Magra to the Rubicon rivers north of the Apennines in Tuscany and Emilia Romagna This definition does not include the Po Valley or the southern slopes of the Alps Nearly all of the peninsula is part of the Italian Republic apart from San Marino and the Vatican City
