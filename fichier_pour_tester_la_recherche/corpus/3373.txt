The Emeishan Traps is a flood basalt volcano volcanic province in Southwestern China around Sichuan province Many igneous rock types can be found there This is known as large igneous province in geology Other names for the traps include Permian Emeishan Large Igneous Province and other variations The Traps are the step like rock made up of many layers of basalt which were laid down by successive eruptions of magma The volcanic eruption eruptions that lead to the Emeshian traps began about million years ago The traps are much smaller than the Siberian Traps which occurred not long afterwards about million years ago Despite this the Emeshian traps were large enough to have an impact on the ecology of the time and on paleontology They are part of the List of extinction events mass extinction events towards the end of the Permian period The Emeishan Traps are part of the scientific debate on the causes of mass extictions This hypothesis is not widely supported
