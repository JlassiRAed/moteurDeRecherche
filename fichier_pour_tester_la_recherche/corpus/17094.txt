 Battery Park City is a acre of dirt and rocks excavated during the construction of the World Trade Center and certain other construction projects as well as from sand dredged from New York Harbor off Staten Island The neighborhood which is the site of the World Financial Center along with many housing commercial and retail buildings is named for adjacent Battery Park Battery Park City is owned and managed by the Battery Park City Authority a public benefit corporation created by New York State under the authority of the Urban Development Corporation Excess revenue from the area was to be contributed to other housing efforts typically low income projects in the Bronx and Harlem
