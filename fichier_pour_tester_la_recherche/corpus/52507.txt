 Blood pressure is a measurement used in medicine In the body the arteries carry blood away from the heart As blood travels through the arteries it presses against the walls of the arteries Blood pressure measures how hard the blood is pushing against the walls of the arteries Usually blood pressure measures the pressure in larger arteries delivering blood to body parts other than the lung s like the brachial artery in the arm Blood pressure is usually measured There are two numbers in a blood pressure The first is the systolic pressure It measures how hard the blood pushes against the walls of the arteries when the heart is in Systole For example in the picture on this page the pressure on the walls of the arteries is mmHg when the heart is beating and mmHg when the heart is resting This blood pressure would be written as and read as over Mean arterial pressure is a person s average blood pressure Pulse pressure is the difference between the systolic and diastolic pressures To measure blood pressure   Medical doctor doctors  use a device called a  sphygmomanometer
