 Old Bet was a female African elephant She was not the first but the second elephant in America She was the first to be known by a name She was about four years old when she arrived in Boston on June She was owned by Edward Savage and exhibited by him in He sold her to Hachaliah Bailey for a reported Bailey sold her but bought her back He leased her to others for exhibition for the rest of her life On June she appeared with the Cayetano and Company circus in New York City and thus became the first elephant to be displayed in a circus in America She was exhibited for years and earned a considerable amount of money Old Bet was shot and killed in Shapleigh Maine on June by Daniel Davis a farmer who claimed the elephant was taking dollars out of the local economy Bailey raised a monument to Old Bet at his Elephant Hotel in Somers New York Her skeleton was displayed in New York City in and was later acquired by P T Barnum for his American Museum
