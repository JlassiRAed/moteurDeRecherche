In statistics a null hypothesis is what you expect to happen before you run an experiment The idea is that if the results don t reject the null hypothesis then you aren t finding anything new or surprising The most common null hypothesis is the no change or no difference hypothesis For example if you re testing whether a thing works and starting with the null hypothesis that it won t work The term was first used by Ronald Fisher in his book The design of experiments Every experiment has a null hypothesis The opposite of a null hypothesis is an alternative hypothesis Some examples of alternative hypotheses are
