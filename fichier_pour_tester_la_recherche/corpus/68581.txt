The Golden temple is the holiest shrines of Sikhs Its dome was decorated with gold on its surface Building was started in the reign of Mughal emperor Akbar He gave the land to the fifth sikh guru Arjun More than people visit it each year Sri Harmandir Sahib  is the official name of the Golden Temple  It is the holiest  Gurdwara  of  Sikhism It is in the city of  Amritsar   Punjab India The fifth Sikh Guru   Guru Arjan designed the Harmandir Sahib to be built and put the  Adi Granth the holy scripture of  Sikhism inside the Harmandir Sahib  The Harmandir Sahib is regarded as the abode of God s spiritual attribute but the Akal Takht is the seat of God s temporal authority The construction of Harmandir Sahib was intended to build a place of worship for men and women from all walks of life and all religions to come and worship God equally  The four entrances regardless of any distinctions a tradition that is a hallmark of all Sikh  Gurudwara Gurudwaras The present day  gurdwara  was rebuilt in by  Jassa Singh Ahluwalia  with the help of other Sikh  Misl Misls In the early nineteenth century   Ranjit Singh Maharaja Ranjit Singh  secured the  Punjab region  from outside attack and covered the upper floors of the gurdwara with  gold which gives it its distinctive appearance and its English name
