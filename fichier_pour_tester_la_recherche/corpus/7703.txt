 Cryptosporidium is a protozoa n parasite which causes outbreaks of diarrhea A number of species of Cryptosporidium can infect mammals The most common species to infect man is Cryptosporidium parvum The diarrhea can become severe in children and in people whose immune system is weakened It is often found in HIV patients who have diarrhea In healthy adults the infection is usually brief It is difficult to identify in the laboratory The oocytes can be seen under a microscope but they may be confused New real time monitoring can detect Cryptosporidium better than the older spot testing and batch testing methods If Cryptosporidium is suspected in the water the safest option is to boil water used for drinking BBC News
