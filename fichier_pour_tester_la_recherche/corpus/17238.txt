The hyoid is a horseshoe shaped bone in the front of the neck between the chin and the thyroid cartilage At rest it lies at the level of the mandible in the front and the third cervical vertebra behind Unlike other bones the hyoid is not directly connected to other bones It is connected only to muscles and ligament s The hyoid aids in tongue movement and swallowing The hyoid bone provides attachment to the muscles of the floor of the mouth and the tongue above the larynx below and the epiglottis and pharynx behind
