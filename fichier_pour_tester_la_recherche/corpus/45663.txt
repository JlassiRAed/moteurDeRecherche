 Theophrastus a Greek native of Eresos in Lesbos was the successor to Aristotle in the Peripatetic school Peripatetic school He came to Athens at a young age and studied in Plato s school After Plato s death he attached himself to Aristotle Aristotle bequeathed to Theophrastus his writings and designated him as his successor at the Lyceum Theophrastus led the Peripatetic school for thirty six years during which time the school flourished greatly He is often considered the father of botany for his works on plant s After his death the Athenians honoured him with a public funeral His successor as head of the school was Strato of Lampsacus category th century BC births category rd century BC deaths category Ancient Greek philosophers
