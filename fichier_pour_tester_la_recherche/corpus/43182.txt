The Second in Command is the second commander the person that is in charge after the commander of any British Army or Royal Marines British Royal Marines unit group He or she is the same as an Executive Officer in the United States Army This term or word is also used in many other Commonwealth of Nations Commonwealth militaries The Second in Command of a group is usually a Major The Second in Command of a smaller group is usually a Captain the Second in Command of an even smaller group is the Sergeant of the group and the Second in Command of a section is usually a Lance Corporal In the Royal Navy the second in command of a vessel or ship is known as the First Lieutenant or Executive Officer
