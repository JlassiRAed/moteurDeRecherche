 Winter storms or snowstorms happen when warm wet air meets with cold air The warm wet air mass and the cold air mass can each be kilometre km or more in diameter Snowstorms affecting Northeastern United States often get their water moisture from air moving north from the Gulf of Mexico and cold air from air masses coming down from the Arctic In the Northwest United States warm wet air from the Pacific Ocean cools when it is pushed upward by the mountain s Many different things can affect the direction of movement content and temperature of air masses All of these differences affect the type and severity of the snowstorm Winter storms and blizzard s can make several feet of snow that blow into big drifts Sometimes the drifts can be over ten feet tall They could even cover a house
