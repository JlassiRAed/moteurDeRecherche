 Pharisees were Jewish leaders who composed and enforced halakha religious law at a certain period in Jewish history At times the Pharisees were a political party a social movement and a school of thought After the Siege of Jerusalem destruction of the Second Temple in AD Pharisaic beliefs became the foundation for Rabbinic Judaism The term Judaism today almost always refers to Rabbinic Judaism Paul of Tarsus disciple of Jesus was a Pharisee prior to his religious conversion to Christianity
