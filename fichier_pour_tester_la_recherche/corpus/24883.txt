 When cut in open one can see four more squares alternately coloured pink and yellow These coloured sections are made by dyeing half of the cake mixture pink and half yellow then cutting each resultant sponge into two long uniform cuboids and joining them together with apricot jam to form one cake Established variations are for coconut flavouring to the sponge cake and lemon curd or raspberry jam in place of apricot jam The cake was probably named in honour of the marriage in of Princess Victoria of Hesse and by Rhine Queen Victoria s granddaughter to Prince Louis of Battenberg with the four squares representing the four Battenberg princes Prince Louis of Battenberg Louis Alexander Prince of Bulgaria Alexander Prince Henry of Battenberg Henry and Prince Francis Joseph of Battenberg Francis Joseph
