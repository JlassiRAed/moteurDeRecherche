 Shinty a traditional game now mainly played in the Scotland Scottish Highlands It involves teams of twelve players using a stick to put a ball in the opposing team s net It was once played over all Scotland but is now played mostly in the Scottish Highlands and Highland communities in the big Scottish cities It is also played in universities and in United States America by enthusiasts The sport s rules and regulations are decided by the Camanachd Association It has a common ancestor with the Irish game of hurling and hurlers and shinty players sometimes hold internationals
