 Lake Dey Dey is a salt lake in South Australia It is one of many salt lakes in the eastern end of the Great Victoria Desert It is normally dry except during and after periods of heavy rain fall It is part of the Basin geological basin known as the Officer Basin The larger Lake Maurice is to the south Since Lake Dey Dey is part of the lands belonging to the Maralinga Tjarutja a southern branch of the Pitjantjatjara The surface of the lake normally consists of dry clay silt or sand covered with a Salt plain salty crust The lake is culturally important to the Maralinga Tjarutja people
