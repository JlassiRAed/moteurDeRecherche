The Davy lamp is a safety lamp for use in mines It was invented in by Sir Humphry Davy It is a Candle wick wick lamp with the flame enclosed inside a mesh screen It was created for use in coal mine s where explosions happened in the presence of methane and other flammable gases Those gases called firedamp or minedamp did not ignite a shielded flame The wire mesh put flames out The lamp also warned the men of carbon dioxide or methane The flame would burn higher and with a blue tinge Modern versions of the lamp are still used to test mine gases but the light for working is now electric
