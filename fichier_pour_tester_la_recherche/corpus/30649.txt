 Vespasian was Roman Emperor from AD to Vespasian was the founder of the Flavian dynasty which ruled the empire for a quarter century Although he held the Roman consul consulship in AD Vespasian became more highly regarded as a successful military commander He took part in the Roman invasion of Britain in AD While Vespasian was preparing to besiege Jerusalem during the latter campaign emperor Nero committed suicide plunging the empire into a year of civil war known as the Year of the Four Emperors After the emperors Galba and Otho died in quick succession Vitellius became emperor in April AD In response the armies in Egypt and Judea Judaea declared Vespasian emperor on July In his bid for imperial power Vespasian joined forces with Gaius Licinius Mucianus Mucianus the governor of Syria and Marcus Antonius Primus Primus a general in Pannonia Primus and Mucianus led the Flavian forces against Vitellius while Vespasian gained control of Egypt On December Vitellius was defeated and the following day Vespasian was declared emperor by the Roman Senate Little factual information survives about Vespasian s government during the ten years he was emperor His reign is best known for financial reforms the successful campaign against Judaea and several ambitious construction projects such as the Colosseum Upon his death in he was succeeded by his eldest son Titus
