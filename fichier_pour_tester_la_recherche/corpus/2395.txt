 Brother Can You Spare a Dime also sung as Buddy Can You Spare a Dime is one of the best known American songs of the Great Depression Written in by lyricist E Y Yip Harburg and composer Jay Gorney Brother Can You Spare a Dime was part of the musical theater musical New Americana the melody is based on a Russian lullaby Gorney heard as a child It became best known however through recordings by Bing Crosby and Rudy Vallee Both versions were released right before Franklin Delano Roosevelt s election to the presidency and both became number one hits on the charts The Brunswick Records Brunswick Crosby recording became the best selling record of its period and came to be viewed as an anthem of the shattered dreams of the era
