 Myoglobin is a protein that is able to bind oxygen Myoglobin which is similar to haemoglobin is the main oxygen carrying biological pigment pigment of muscle tissues Like haemoglobin it has a aromatic hydrocarbon porphyrin ring with an iron atom at its centre It is in the muscle Tissue tissue of most mammal s Pinniped Seal s and other ocean marine mammal s have more myoglobin in their muscles than land animals In humans increased level of myoglobin in the serum of the blood can be an indication of myocardial infarction Other factors for a higher level can be damage to the muscle s epilepsy epileptic seizure s polytrauma If the value is much too high this can lead to kidney failure
