 Provenance from the French provenir to come from refers to the history of ownership or the position of a historical object The term was originally used in relation to works of art It is now used in a wide range of fields including archaeology paleontology archive s manuscript s printed books and science and computing The main reason to trace the provenance of an object is to get evidence of its original production or discovery This has a special value to help Authentication authenticate objects to prove what they are Provenance is essentially a matter of Document documentation In archaeology the term provenience is used in related but a subtly different sense to provenance Archaeological researchers use provenience to refer to the three dimensional location or find spot of an artifact or feature within an archaeological site whereas provenance covers an object s complete documented history Ideally in modern excavations the provenience or find spot is recorded In some cases especially where there is an inscription the provenance may include a history that predates its burial in the ground as well as its history after rediscovery
