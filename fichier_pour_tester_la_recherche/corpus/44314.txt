 Ostracods are a Class class of the Crustacea They are often called seed shrimps because of their appearance Some species have been identified Ostracods are small crustaceans typically around in the case of Gigantocypris Their bodies are flattened from side to side and protected by a bivalve like chitin ous or calcareous valve or shell The hinge of the two valves is in the upper region of the body Marine ostracods live in the zooplankton Others live in the benthos on or in the upper layer of the sea floor Many ostracods are also found in fresh water Land Terrestrial species of Mesocypris are known from humid forest soil s of South Africa Australia New Zealand and Tasmania They have a wide range of diet s and the group includes carnivore s herbivore s scavenger s and filter feeders Copepods may not be monophyletic
