 Rosh Chodesh or Rosh Hodesh is the name for the first day of every month in the Hebrew calendar marked by the birth of a new moon It is considered a minor holiday like the middle days of Passover and Sukkot When a Hebrew calendar month is days long day is considered Rosh Chodesh of the next month Then Rosh Chodesh is two days long day of the old month and day of the new month
