In computing a trackball is a pointing device pointing input device It consists of a Sphere ball held by a socket containing sensors to detect a rotation of the ball about two axes It is like an upside down computer mouse mouse with a ball that sticks out The user rolls the ball with the thumb finger s or the palm of the hand to move a cursor cursor They are currently only made by major companies Logitech ATech and Kensington Microsoft also used to be a major producer Large trackballs are sometimes seen on computerized special purpose workstations such as the radar consoles in an air traffic control room or sonar equipment on a ship or submarine
