The white bellied sea eagle is also known as the white breasted sea eagle It is a large diurnal daytime bird of prey in the family Accipitridae It is closely related to Sanford s sea eagle of the Solomon Islands It is a distinctive bird with a white belly white head breast under wing covert coverts and tail The upper parts are grey The black under wing flight feathers contrast with the white coverts The tail is short and wedge shaped as in all sea eagle Haliaeetus species Like many raptors the female is slightly larger than the male and can be Immature birds have brown plumage which is gradually replaced with white by the age of five or six years Its call is a loud goose like honking The bird lives in India and Sri Lanka through Southeast Asia to Australia on coasts and major waterways It breeds and hunts near water and fish form around half of its diet Opportunistic it eats carrion and a wide variety of animals Although rated of Least Concern globally it has declined in parts of southeast Asia It is ranked as Threatened in Victoria Victoria and Vulnerable in South Australia and Tasmania Human disturbance to its habitat is the main threat The white bellied sea eagle is revered by Indigenous Australians indigenous people in many parts of Australia and is the subject of various folk tales throughout its range
