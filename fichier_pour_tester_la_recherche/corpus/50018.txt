In a light bulb there is a small thin wire with two bigger wires holding it up This wire is called a filament The filament is the part of the light bulb that produces light Filaments in incandescent light bulbs are made of tungsten When electric current goes through the filament the filament glows It may also be known as the electron emitting element in a vacuum tube To make the bulb produce more light the filament is usually made of coils of fine wire also known as the coiled coil
