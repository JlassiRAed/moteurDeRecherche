 Wood carving is a form of Woodworking working wood by means of a cutting tool A chisel or knife are usual tools the chisel can be tapped with a wooden mallet The result is a wooden figure or the sculpture sculptural ornamentation of a wooden object A wood carving may also refer to the finished product The making of sculpture in wood has a long history Wood survives much less well than the other materials such as stone and bronze It is vulnerable to decay insect damage and fire Therefore we do not know much about wood in the art history of older cultures Outdoor wood sculptures do not last long in most parts of the world so that we have little idea how the totemism totem pole tradition developed Many of the most important sculptures of China and Japan are in wood also most Africa n sculpture and that of Oceania and other regions Wood is light so it is suitable for mask s and other objects meant to be carried It can take very fine detail and is also much easier to work than stone Some of the finest examples of early European wood carving are from the Middle Ages in Germany Russia Italy and France where the typical themes of that era were used in Christian icon s In England many complete examples remain from the th and th century when oak was often used In general really fine carving is done in fruitwood because that is easy to work with The famous woodcarver Grinling Gibbons used to work with tilia lime wood Complex pieces are often carved in separate sections which are later stuck together
