 Penryn is a small town in south Cornwall United Kingdom It is on the Penryn River about one mile northwest of Falmouth The population was in the census Penryn was in the Middle Ages an important port but underwent a severe decline after Falmouth had been established in the th century Today it is a busy town and has managed to retain a much of its heritage a large proportion of its buildings date back to Tudor Jacobean and Georgian times the town is an important conservation area and has more listed buildings than any other town in Cornwall
