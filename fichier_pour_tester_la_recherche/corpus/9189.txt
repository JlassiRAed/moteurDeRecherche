 The sugar glider is a small marsupial originally native to eastern and northern mainland Australia New Guinea and the Bismarck Archipelago and introduced to Tasmania It is called a sugar glider because it likes to feed on the sugary sap from certain trees and can jump from trees and glide through the air to another tree They live in trees and rarely travel on the ground They eat many foods but mostly tree sap and insects They look and act much like a flying squirrel but they are not related Sugar gliders are actually related to possums
