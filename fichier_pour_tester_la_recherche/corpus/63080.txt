 St Columb Major is a small town in Cornwall United Kingdom Often referred to locally as St Columb it is about seven miles east of Newquay The full name distinguishes it from the village and parish of St Columb Minor on the coast Twice a year the town plays host to the game of hurling a medieval game once common in Cornwall but now only played in St Columb and two other towns It is played on Shrove Tuesday and then again on the Saturday eleven days later The game involves two teams of several hundred people apart making the parish the largest pitch for a ball game anywhere in the world
