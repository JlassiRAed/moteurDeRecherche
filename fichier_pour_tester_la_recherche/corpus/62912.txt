Rabi crop are grown or sown  in  winter  and harvested in the spring in the  South Asia rabi word if obtained from Arabic language which means spring It is also called rabi harvest and spring harvest The rabi crops are sown around mid November after the  monsoon  rains are over and harvesting begins in April May The crops are grown either with rainwater that has  Percolation percolated  into the ground or with  irrigation A good rain in winter spoils the rabi crop but is good for kharif crops Normally wheat barley   Mustard seed mustard   sesame  and  peas are grown in india  Peas are harvested early as they are ready early Indian markets are flooded with green peas from January to March peaking in February Some of the example are Cereals Seed plants Vegetables
