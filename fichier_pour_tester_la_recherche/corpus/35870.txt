The House of Commons of Canada Members are elected for a maximum of four years at a time Each member is elected by one of the country s federal electoral districts which are usually called ridings The House of Commons was established in when the British North America Act created the Dominion of Canada and was modelled on the House of Commons of the United Kingdom British House of Commons The House of Commons is sometimes called the lower house even though it has more power than the upper house the Senate Both Houses is must agree to new laws but the Senate very rarely rejects bills passed by the Commons The Government of Canada is responsible only to the House of Commons The Prime Minister of Canada Prime Minister stays in office only as long as he or she has the support of the Lower House The Canadian House of Commons is in the Centre Block of the Parliament Buildings on Parliament Hill in Ottawa Ontario Unlike the Parliament of the United Kingdom the powers of the Parliament of Canada are limited because provincial legislatures have the sole right to pass laws about some things Until only the Parliament of the United Kingdom had the power to change the British North America Act this was to protect the rights and powers of the provincial legislatures This was changed by the Canada Act In Canada the British North America Act is now called the Constitution Act A lot of the work of the House of Commons is done by committees which can spend more time investigating a subject than the whole House of Commons could
