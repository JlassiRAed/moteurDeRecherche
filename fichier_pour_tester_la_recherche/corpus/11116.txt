The Rivonia Trial was a trial that took place in South Africa between and Ten leaders of the African National Congress were tried for acts of sabotage designed to overthrow the apartheid system This trial was held after the police raid on the MK base in Rivonia where documents related to the accused men were found One of the men was the future President of South Africa Nelson Mandela After the trial Mandela was taken to jail in Robben Island The chief prosecutor was Dr Percy Yutar deputy attorney general of the Transvaal Province Transvaal The presiding judge was Dr Quartus de Wet judge president of the Transvaal Province Transvaal
