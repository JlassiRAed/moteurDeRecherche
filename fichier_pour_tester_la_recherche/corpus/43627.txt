A type species is the species that contains the biological type specimen International Code of Zoological Nomenclature th ed adopted by the International Union of Biological Sciences International Commission on Zoological Nomenclature Article The type system is central to the scientific naming of living things There are international Codes of nomenclature which set out exactly how types are to be designated and used For animals the type species of a genus and a type specimen For algae fungi and plants it is different the type species of a genus is the species that has the same type as the genus For example Malus sylvestris the European Wild Apple is the type species of the genus Malus Thus for algae fungi and plants a type species plays no great role it is not an official term but only a term of convenience
