A map scale is the size of an object compared to the size of the object s smaller representative on a map This can be shown by a Scale scale bar and a ratio n This lets the person using the map measure a distance on the map and know what is the distance on the ground People sometimes speak of a map as one with a large scale or small scale A large scale map makes things look large and a small scale map makes things look small For example an island displayed on a map will appear larger than if it were displayed on a map Thus the former is large scale What can be confusing is that for a map of a given physical size say by inches a large scale map will have a smaller geographic extent than a small scale map centred on the same point Maps with a ratio of or larger
