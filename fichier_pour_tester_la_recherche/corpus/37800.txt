 Gyrwas was the name of an early Anglo Saxon people They lived mainly on the western edge of the grassy wetlands called the Fens in eastern England They were divided into two tribal groups the northern Gyrwas and the southern Gyrwas This is how they were recorded in the Tribal Hidage The name Gyrwe means fen dweller The Gyrwas had their own leaders as late as the first half of the th century For a time the territory of the Gyrwas formed a en wikt buffer buffer state between the Mercia Mercians and the East Anglia East Anglians The Gyrwas were later absorbed into Middle Angles Middle Anglia
