The Olduvai Gorge is a steep sided ravine in the Great Rift Valley that stretches through East Africa It is in the eastern Serengeti Serengeti Plains in northern Tanzania The Gorge is about long It is km from the Laetoli archaeological site The gorge is a highly important prehistoric site for the study of human evolution It was once a lake and got covered by layers of volcanic ash This site was occupied by Homo habilis about million years ago Paranthropus boisei million years ago and Homo erectus million years ago Homo sapiens occupied the site years ago The name is a misspelling of Oldupai Gorge which was adopted as the official name in Oldupai is the Maasai language Maasai word for the wild sisal plant Sansevieria ehrenbergii which grows in the gorge
