 Salamanders are amphibians They are the order There are about different species Salamanders live in water and on land and look rather like lizard s with four legs and a tail They hatch as larvae from eggs in the water At that stage they have gills that stick out Most salamanders live on land when they are adults after their change of shape On land salamanders live in moist habitat s Their method of respiration varies Some salamanders stay in the water but look like adults These are called newt s Some salamanders retain gills into sexual maturity such as the Axolotl This process is called neoteny Often salamanders may have a chemical defence to predators they are poisonous to eat In this case they will have warning colouration Otherwise if not poisonous they will usually have camouflage Salamanders are the only tetrapods able to regeneate limbs as well as other body parts
