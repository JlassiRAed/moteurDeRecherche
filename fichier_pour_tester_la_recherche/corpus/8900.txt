 William Langland was an English poet He wrote a long and complicated poem called Piers Plowman The poem is an allegory about the struggle to lead a virtuous Christian life when the institution of the Church is often corrupt We do not know for sure that this poem was written by Langland The strongest evidence we have is one manuscript which says that the poem was written by Willielmi de Langlond son of Stacy de Rokayle who died in Shipton under Wichwood a tenant of the Lord Spenser in the county of Oxfordshire Other manuscripts also name the author as Robert or William langland or Wilhelmus W Another piece of evidence is within the poem itself At one stage the narrator says I have lyved in londe my name is longe wille Professor Scholars agree that this is a code for the poet s name Longe Land and Wille meaning Will short for William This may seem unlikely but this method of hiding the poet s name within a poem was used in Ancient Rome Roman times and was quite common in late medieval literature Long Willy was a contemporary of Chaucer the master of the pen
