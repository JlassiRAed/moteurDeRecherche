 Geomorphology is the scientific study of landform s and the processes that shape them More broadly it studies the evolution of the topography of a planet Geomorphologists seek to understand why landscape s look the way they do to understand landform history and dynamics and to predict future changes They use a combination of field observation physical experiment and Computer model numerical modeling Geomorphology is part of geography geology geodesy engineering geology and archaeology This range of interests leads to a wide variety of research styles and interests in the field
