 Nostratic is a hypothesis hypothetical language family which includes many of the present day language families of Eurasia The idea is that Nostratic was spoken after the ice age ice sheets melted but before people spread out throughout Europe and Asia Many of today s languages are descendants of Nostratic so it is thought These include the Indo European languages Indo European Uralic languages Uralic Altaic languages Altaic and Kartvelian languages Kartvelian languages Usually also included are the Afroasiatic languages native to North Africa the Horn of Africa the Arabian Peninsula and the Near East and the Dravidian languages of the Indian subcontinent The name Nostratic is derived from the Latin wikt nostrates nostrates fellow countrymen The idea was expanded in the s by Soviet linguists called the Moscovite school by Bomhard It has received renewed attention in English speaking academia since the s The hypothesis is controversial and has varying degrees of acceptance amongst linguists worldwide Some linguists take an agnostic view
