 Sapphic stanza is a strophe that is widely used in European poetry It is of Greece Greek origin It is named after the ancient Greek woman poet Sappho The stanza consists of four lines Three of them are composed of eleven syllable s and the last one is made up of five syllables In Greek verse the quantity of syllables was important That is whether the syllables are long or short In other languages it is no longer significant Sapphic stanza was adopted by Roman poets for example by Horace
