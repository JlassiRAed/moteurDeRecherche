A flageolet is an old woodwind instrument It belongs to the end blown flute family It started to be used in the th century for folk music Soon there were two kinds the France French kind and the England English The French flageolet had four holes in the front and two in the back The English flageolet had six holes in the front The flageolet is quite similar to the recorder In the th century it changed because a narrow mouthpiece made of ivory or bone was added at the top This led into another section which bulged out There was a soft sponge at the end of this Sometimes this instrument was called the flautino The piccolo developed from it Another instrument that developed from it was the tin whistle Henry Purcell and George Frideric Handel both wrote pieces for it Small versions of this instrument called bird flageolets were also made and were used for teaching birds to sing
